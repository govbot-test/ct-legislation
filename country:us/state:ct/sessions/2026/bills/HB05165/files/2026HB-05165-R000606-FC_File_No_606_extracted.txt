AN ACT REQUIRING AN EVALUATION OF THE APPOINTMENT OF A
RECEIVER TO MANAGE HOSPITALS IN FINANCIAL DISTRESS OR
OPERATIONAL CRISIS.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General
Assembly convened:
1 Section 1. (Effective from passage) (a) As used in this section, "a hospital
2 in financial distress or operational crisis" means a hospital that (1) has
3 filed for bankruptcy protection, or (2) is experiencing three or more of
4 the following conditions: (A) The hospital has been issued a negative
5 going concern opinion by its independent auditing firm; (B) the hospital
6 owes fees, taxes or an assessment to one or more public entities and such
7 fees or taxes are, or such assessment is, not less than one hundred eighty
8 days past due, provided the hospital has not (i) commenced a
9 proceeding to contest the payment of such fees, taxes or assessment, or
10 (ii) been granted an extension of time to pay such fees, taxes or
11 assessment; (C) the hospital is in default of a material covenant on a
12 bond contract or similar debt instrument; (D) the hospital has failed to
13 make a payment of wages, as defined in section 31-71a of the general

State Impact: None

Explanation
The bill requires the Department of Public Health (DPH)
Commissioner to evaluate whether the Attorney General should be
authorized to petition the Superior Court to appoint a receiver to
manage hospitals in financial distress or operational crisis, resulting in
no fiscal impact. The department has the necessary expertise to conduct
the evaluation and reporting required by the bill.
House "A" makes clarifying changes to the underlying bill, resulting
in no fiscal impact.
