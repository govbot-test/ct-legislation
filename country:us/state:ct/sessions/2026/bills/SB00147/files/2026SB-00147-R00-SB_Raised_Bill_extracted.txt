General Assembly Raised Bill No. 147
February Session, 2026 LCO No. 839
Referred to Committee on ENVIRONMENT
Introduced by:
(ENV)
AN ACT CONCERNING A STUDY ON THE NEED FOR EXTENDED
PRODUCER RESPONSIBILITY FOR SOLAR PANELS AND VAPES
AND INCLUDING AEROSOL PAINTS UNDER THE PAINT
STEWARDSHIP PROGRAM.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General
Assembly convened:
1 Section 1. (Effective from passage) The Commissioner of Energy and
2 Environmental Protection shall study the need to establish extended
3 producer responsibility programs in the state for discarded solar panels
4 and vapes, respectively, to provide for the recycling and potential reuse
5 of the materials, including, but not limited to, rare minerals and metals,
6 used in the manufacture of such panels and to limit or prevent any fire
7 or explosive hazard that may be associated with the disposal of such
8 vaping devices. Such study shall include, but need not be limited to, an
9 assessment of the volume and estimated weight of solar panels, for
10 commercial and residential installations in the state, that will reach the
11 end of their useful life in the next five-year period, as well as the
12 circumstances under which a disposed vaping device poses a fire or
13 explosion risk. Additionally, such study shall determine: (1) What
14 procedures are currently used for the collection, disposal and recycling

34 Sec. 2. Subdivision (1) of section 22a-904 of the general statutes is
35 repealed and the following is substituted in lieu thereof (Effective from
36 passage):
37 (1) "Architectural paint" means interior and exterior architectural
38 coatings sold in containers of five gallons or less and includes any such
39 aerosol or spray paint. "Architectural paint" does not include industrial,
40 original equipment or specialty coatings.
