AN ACT CONCERNING CONVEYANCE OF PARCELS OF STATE
LAND IN THE TOWN OF DERBY.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General
Assembly convened:
1 Section 1. (Effective from passage) (a) Notwithstanding any provision
2 of the general statutes, the Commissioner of Transportation shall convey
3 to the Connecticut Municipal Development Authority parcels of land
4 located in the town of Derby, at a cost equal to the fair market value of
5 the property, as determined by the average of the appraisals of two
6 independent appraisers selected by the commissioner, plus the
7 administrative costs of making such conveyance. Said parcels of land
8 have an area of approximately 1.5 acres and are identified as Lots 178,
9 181, 182, 183, 184, 185, 190, 191, 193 and 211 on town of Derby Tax
10 Assessor's Map 8-5 and Lot 7 on town of Derby Tax Assessor's Map 8-3.
11 The precise size and area of the parcels to be conveyed shall be
12 determined by the Commissioner of Transportation. The conveyance
13 shall be subject to the approval of the State Properties Review Board.

Explanation
The bill conveys several parcels of land, totaling 1.5 acres, from the
Department of Transportation (DOT) to the Connecticut Municipal
Development Authority (CMDA).
This results in a transfer of assets from DOT to CMDA, with CMDA
paying the fair market value and administrative costs of the transfer.
DOT would lose use of the asset and gain revenues equal to the fair
market value of the property to the STF. CMDA would gain use of the
asset and would pay fair market value of the property and the
administrative costs of the conveyance.
The land conveyance is subject to the review and approval of the State
Properties Review Board.
House "A" adds a requirement that CMDA pay fair market value of
the property and removes a requirement that CMDA develop, sell, or
lease the property within ten years or have the property revert to DOT
