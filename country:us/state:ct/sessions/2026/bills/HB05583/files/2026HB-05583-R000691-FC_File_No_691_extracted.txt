Explanation
The bill conveys several parcels of land, totaling 1.5 acres, from the
Department of Transportation (DOT) to the Connecticut Municipal
Development Authority (CMDA).
This results in a transfer of assets from DOT to CMDA, with CMDA
paying the administrative costs of the transfer. The assets would revert
to DOT control if CMDA does not use the parcels for specified purposes
within ten years.
The bill allows CMDA to sell or lease the parcels, provided the
proceeds of which are deposited into the Special Transportation Fund
(STF). Any sale of the parcels would result in the loss of asset for CMDA.
Any sale or lease would result in a gain of revenue for the STF.
