AN ACT ESTABLISHING A TASK FORCE TO STUDY ISSUES
RELATING TO CONSUMER PROTECTION AND OCCUPATIONAL
LICENSING.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General
Assembly convened:
1 Section 1. (Effective from passage) (a) There is established a task force
2 to study issues relating to consumer protection and occupational
3 licensing.
4 (b) The task force shall consist of the following members:
5 (1) Two appointed by the speaker of the House of Representatives;
6 (2) Two appointed by the president pro tempore of the Senate;
7 (3) One appointed by the majority leader of the House of
8 Representatives;
9 (4) One appointed by the majority leader of the Senate;
10 (5) One appointed by the minority leader of the House of
11 Representatives;

12 (6) One appointed by the minority leader of the Senate;
13 (7) The Commissioner of Consumer Protection, or the commissioner's
14 designee; and
15 (8) Two persons appointed by the Governor.
16 (c) Any member of the task force appointed under subdivision (1),
17 (2), (3), (4), (5) or (6) of subsection (b) of this section may be a member
18 of the General Assembly.
19 (d) All initial appointments to the task force shall be made not later
20 than thirty days after the effective date of this section. Any vacancy shall
21 be filled by the appointing authority.
22 (e) The speaker of the House of Representatives and the president pro
23 tempore of the Senate shall select the chairpersons of the task force from
24 among the members of the task force. Such chairpersons shall schedule
25 the first meeting of the task force, which shall be held not later than sixty
26 days after the effective date of this section.
27 (f) The administrative staff of the joint standing committee of the
28 General Assembly having cognizance of matters relating to consumer
29 protection shall serve as administrative staff of the task force.
30 (g) Not later than January 1, 2027, the task force shall submit a report
31 on its findings and recommendations to the joint standing committee of
32 the General Assembly having cognizance of matters relating to
33 consumer protection, in accordance with the provisions of section 11-4a
34 of the general statutes. The task force shall terminate on the date that it
35 submits such report or January 1, 2027, whichever is later.
This act shall take effect as follows and shall amend the following
sections:
