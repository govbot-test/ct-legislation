Explanation
The resolution places a proposed amendment on either the
November 2026 or November 2028 ballot to establish in the Constitution
an individual right to clean and healthy air, water, soil, and ecosystems,
resulting in a cost to the Secretary of the State (SOTS) of $25,000 in either
FY 27 or FY 29.
The timing of the cost is dependent on when the requirements for the
resolution's passage are met. The resolution must pass by three-quarters
of the General Assembly for the cost to be incurred in FY 27. If the
requisite votes are not met, but the resolution is approved by a majority
in that session and the next, then the cost to SOTS will shift to FY 29.
The cost to SOTS for placing the proposed amendment on the ballot
includes printing and postage for distributing the amendment to
municipalities for inclusion.
The Out Years
The SOTS cost of placing the amendment on the ballot may occur in
FY 29, as described above.

SUMMARY
This resolution proposes a state constitutional amendment to give
each person an individual right to clean and healthy air, water, soil,
ecosystems, and environment and a safe and stable climate for the
benefit of public health, safety, and the general welfare. It prohibits
anyone from infringing on these rights and requires the state to protect
them equitably for all people regardless of race, ethnicity, tribal
affiliation, gender, socioeconomic status, or geography. It also requires
the state to conserve, protect, and maintain all the state’s natural
resources for its residents (present and future), including its waters, air,
flora, fauna, soils, and climate.
The resolution directs that the state shall not allow, through
government action or inaction, any avoidable degradation, diminution,
or depletion of the natural environment that contributes to significant
or widespread environmental harm, or results in an unhealthy or
unsustainable environment.
Under the resolution, these rights are inalienable, and state residents
may directly invoke and enforce them. The resolution also prohibits
diverting any funds supporting the protection of the state’s natural
resources.
The ballot designation to be used when the amendment is presented
at the general election is: “Shall the Constitution of the State be amended
to (1) provide each citizen an enforceable individual right to clean and
healthy air, water, soil, ecosystems and environment, and a safe and
stable climate for the benefit of public health, safety and the general

EFFECTIVE DATE: If the resolution passes by at least three-fourths
of the membership of each house of the General Assembly, it will be
placed on the 2026 general election ballot. If it passes by a majority of
the membership of each house but less than three-fourths, it will be
referred to the 2027 legislative session. If it passes in that session by a
majority of each house, it will appear on the 2028 general election ballot.
If a majority of those voting on the amendment in the general election
approves it, the amendment will become part of the state constitution.
