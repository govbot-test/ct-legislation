AN ACT ESTABLISHING A TASK FORCE TO STUDY EXPANDING
THE 2-1-1 INFOLINE PROGRAM.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General
Assembly convened:
1 Section 1. (Effective from passage) (a) There is established a task force
2 to study expanding services provided by the 2-1-1 Infoline program
3 operated by the United Way of Connecticut. Such study shall include,
4 but need not be limited to, state funding necessary to expand services of
5 the program.
6 (b) The task force shall consist of the following members:
7 (1) Two representatives of the United Way of Connecticut, appointed
8 by the speaker of the House of Representatives;
9 (2) Two representatives of advocacy organizations who serve clients
10 who have benefited or may benefit from services provided by the 2-1-1
11 Infoline program, appointed by the president pro tempore of the Senate;
12 (3) One appointed by the majority leader of the House of
13 Representatives;

41 (f) The administrative staff of the joint standing committee of the
42 General Assembly having cognizance of matters relating to human
43 services shall serve as administrative staff of the task force.
44 (g) Not later than January 1, 2027, the task force shall submit a report
45 on its findings and recommendations to the joint standing committees
46 of the General Assembly having cognizance of matters relating to
47 appropriations and the budgets of state agencies and human services, in
48 accordance with the provisions of section 11-4a of the general statutes.
49 The report shall include, but need not be limited to, (1) state costs to
50 expand staff of the 2-1-1 Infoline program by twenty per cent, and (2)
51 strategies to reduce wait times for callers to the Infoline to five minutes
52 or less. The task force shall terminate on the date that it submits such
53 report or January 1, 2027, whichever is later.
