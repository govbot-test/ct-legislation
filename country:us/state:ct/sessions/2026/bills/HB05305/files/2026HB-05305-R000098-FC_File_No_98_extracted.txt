Explanation
The bill results in a cost to the Department of Social Services (DSS) of
at least $730,000 in FY 27 and $875,000 in FY 28. These figures reflect a
10% increase in the adult day services fee schedule for FY 27, followed
by annual cost-of-living adjustments tied to the Consumer Price Index
(CPI)1 starting in FY 28.
Based on historical utilization levels, this reflects both the state share
of Medicaid costs (approximately $665,000 in FY 27 and $800,000 in FY
28) and costs under the state-funded Connecticut Home Care Program
(approximately $65,000 in FY 27 and $75,000 in FY 28). The purpose of
the funding is to provide increased support for adult day care
transportation.
The Out Years
The annualized ongoing fiscal impact identified above would
continue into the future subject to service utilization and CPI changes.
1 This is based on the CPI-U of 2.7% for the 12 months ending in December 2025.

OLR Bill Analysis
HB 5305
AN ACT CONCERNING ADULT DAY CENTER TRANSPORTATION.
SUMMARY
Starting July 1, 2026, this bill requires the Department of Social
Services (DSS) commissioner to increase the fee the department pays for
adult day care services by 10% over the previous fiscal year to cover
providers’ transportation costs.
Beginning July 1, 2027, the bill also requires the commissioner to
annually increase the fee for adult day care services by, at minimum,
any cost-of-living increases reflected in the consumer price index.
Existing law allows, but does not require, the commissioner to annually
increase the fee schedule based on increases in service costs.
EFFECTIVE DATE: July 1, 2026
