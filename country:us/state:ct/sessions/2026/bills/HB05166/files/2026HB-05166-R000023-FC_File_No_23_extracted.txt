Explanation
The bill eliminates the examination requirement for master social
worker licensure, resulting in an annual minimal General Fund revenue
gain in license renewal fees beginning in FY 28. The amount of the
revenue gain will depend on the number of additional individuals who
apply for their initial license at no cost and, in FY 28 and beyond,
annually renew their master social worker license for $125 due to this
change. There are currently 5,608 master social workers licensed by the
state.
The Out Years
The annualized ongoing fiscal impact identified above would
continue into the future subject to any change in the license renewal fee.

OLR Bill Analysis
HB 5166
AN ACT CONCERNING SOCIAL WORK LICENSURE.
SUMMARY
This bill eliminates the requirement for a master social worker license
applicant to pass the Association of Social Work Boards’ (ASWB)
master’s level examination, or other examination the Department of
Public Health (DPH) commissioner prescribes. (Existing law
temporarily suspended this requirement between June 7, 2023, and
January 1, 2026.)
It correspondingly eliminates a provision that authorizes DPH to
issue a 120-day temporary permit that allows master social worker
license applicants who have a master’s degree from a social work
program but have not yet taken the licensure examination, to practice
under professional supervision.
Under current law and the bill, applicants for a master social worker
license still must have a master’s degree from a social work program
that is (1) accredited by the Council on Social Work Education (CSWE),
(2) in-state and has candidate status with CSWE during or after the 2024
spring semester and before the 2028 spring semester, or (3) deemed
equivalent by CSWE, if educated outside of the U.S. and its territories.
Applicants for licensure by endorsement must be licensed or certified as
a master social worker in good standing in another state or jurisdiction
with requirements substantially similar or higher than Connecticut’s.
EFFECTIVE DATE: July 1, 2026
BACKGROUND
Social Work Licensure
By law, Connecticut licenses social workers at two levels: master
