General Assembly Substitute Bill No. 5401
February Session, 2026
AN ACT CONCERNING THE PROCEDURE FOR ADOPTING,
REVISING AND AMENDING THE STATE BUILDING CODE AND
REQUIRING RELATED TRAINING.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General
Assembly convened:
1 Section 1. Subsection (a) of section 29-252 of the general statutes is
2 repealed and the following is substituted in lieu thereof (Effective October
3 1, 2026):
4 (a) (1) The State Building Inspector and the Codes and Standards
5 Committee shall, jointly, with the approval of the Commissioner of
6 Administrative Services and in accordance with the provisions of
7 section 29-252b, adopt and administer a State Building Code based on a
8 nationally recognized model building code for the purpose of regulating
9 the design, construction and use of buildings or structures to be erected
10 and the alteration of buildings or structures already erected and make
11 such amendments thereto as they, from time to time, deem necessary or
12 desirable. Such amendments shall be limited to [administrative matters,
13 geotechnical and weather-related portions of said code, amendments to
14 said code necessitated by a provision of the general statutes and any
15 other matter which, based on substantial evidence, necessitates an
16 amendment to said code] amendments necessitated by a state or federal
17 law and amendments necessary to protect public health and safety or
18 maintain eligibility for federal funding. The [code] State Building Code
19 shall be revised as deemed necessary to incorporate any subsequent

53 (5) (A) Notwithstanding any provision of this chapter, from October
54 1, 2026, to October 1, 2032, inclusive, the State Building Inspector and
55 the Codes and Standards Committee shall not require, consider,
56 approve or adopt any new building standard, except for any
57 amendment that the State Building Inspector and the Codes and
58 Standards Committee determine to be necessary to (i) address an
59 imminent threat to public health or life safety, (ii) maintain eligibility
60 for, or avoid the loss of, federal funding, including, but not limited to,
61 disaster recovery funds, (iii) comply with federal law or regulation, (iv)
62 make (I) editorial, formatting or nonsubstantive revisions solely for
63 clarity or internal consistency, or (II) technical corrections that do not
64 increase the cost of construction or expand the scope of activity
65 regulated by the State Building Code, or (v) incorporate minimum
66 federal accessibility requirements. For purposes of this subdivision,
67 "building standard" means any requirement that applies to the
68 construction, alteration, addition, repair, change of use or occupancy of
69 any residential, commercial, institutional, industrial or mixed-use
70 building or structure.
71 (B) Any amendment made pursuant to subparagraph (A) of this
72 subdivision shall (i) be narrowly tailored to the minimum necessary
73 scope, (ii) include written findings and a statement of necessity made by
74 the State Building Inspector and included in the code-making record,
75 and (iii) terminate automatically not later than twenty-four months after
76 the effective date of such amendment unless extended in accordance
77 with this subdivision.
78 (C) Notwithstanding the provisions of section 7-148, during the time
79 period described in subparagraph (A) of this subdivision, no
80 municipality shall adopt, and no local building official shall enforce, any
81 building standard that is more restrictive than the building standards
82 established by the State Building Code.

84 repealed and the following is substituted in lieu thereof (Effective October
85 1, 2026):
