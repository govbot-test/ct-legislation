Asset
Explanation
The bill conveys a specified parcel of land, totaling 0.22 acres, from
the Department of Transportation to the city of Torrington. The state
would lose use of the asset (most recently valued at $29,100). The state
would retain an existing easement for property access. The city of
Torrington would gain use of an asset and would pay the administrative
costs of the conveyance. The asset would revert to state control if the
recipient does not use the land for specified purposes.
The land conveyance is subject to the review and approval of the State
Properties Review Board.
The Out Years
The conveyance action is one time in nature. Control of the asset
would continue into the future as described above, subject to future
municipal decisions.
