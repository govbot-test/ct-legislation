General Assembly Substitute Bill No. 256
February Session, 2026
AN ACT CONCERNING THE PURCHASE OF RESIDENTIAL
PROPERTY BY PRIVATE EQUITY ENTITIES.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General
Assembly convened:
1 Section 1. (NEW) (Effective October 1, 2026) (a) For the purposes of this
2 section:
3 (1) (A) "Private equity entity" means an institutional real estate
4 investor or an entity that receives funding from an institutional real
5 estate investor for the purchase of a single-family residence or two-
6 family residence. A loan provided in exchange for a mortgage of the
7 residence that is being purchased is not considered funding for the
8 purposes of this subdivision, provided such mortgage is of a type for
9 which members of the general public may apply.
10 (B) "Private equity entity" does not include: (i) An organization which
11 is described in Section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986, or
12 any subsequent corresponding internal revenue code of the United
13 States, as amended from time to time, and exempt from tax under
14 Section 501(a) of said Internal Revenue Code, or (ii) a housing land trust
15 program described in sections 8-214b to 8-214e, inclusive, of the general
16 statutes.
17 (2) "Institutional real estate investor" means an entity or combined

28 residence that is to be used as the principal residence of any person who
29 has an ownership interest in the private equity entity that seeks to
30 purchase the single-family residence; or (B) any single-family residence
31 constructed, acquired or operated with federal, state or municipal
32 appropriated funds.
33 (4) "Two-family residence" does not include: (A) Any two-family
34 residence in which one of the dwelling units is to be used as the principal
35 residence of any person who has an ownership interest in the private
36 equity entity that seeks to purchase the two-family residence; or (B) any
37 two-family residence constructed, acquired or operated with federal,
38 state or municipal appropriated funds.
39 (b) (1) No private equity entity may purchase, acquire or offer to
40 purchase or acquire any interest in a single-family residence or two-
41 family residence unless the single-family residence or two-family
42 residence has been listed for sale to the general public for at least ninety
43 days.
44 (2) The ninety-day waiting period set forth in subdivision (1) of this
45 subsection shall restart if the seller changes the asking price for the
46 single-family residence or two-family residence, and a private equity
47 entity shall be prohibited from purchasing, acquiring or offering to
48 purchase or acquire any interest in the single-family residence or two-
49 family residence until it has been listed for sale to the general public at
