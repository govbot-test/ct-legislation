249 Sec. 3. (Effective July 1, 2026) (a) As used in this section, (1) "benefits
250 cliffs" means the loss or reduction of public assistance due to an increase
251 in employment income, and (2) "public assistance" means programs
252 including, but not limited to, temporary family assistance, supplemental
253 nutrition assistance or housing assistance. Within available
254 appropriations, the Department of Social Services and the Office of
255 Early Childhood, in consultation with the Labor Department, the
256 Department of Housing, the Office of Workforce Strategy and the two-
257 generational initiative established pursuant to section 17b-112l of the
258 general statutes, shall enter into a public-private partnership to establish
259 a two-year pilot program to mitigate benefits cliffs for two hundred
260 households receiving public assistance.
261 (b) The pilot program established pursuant to subsection (a) of this
262 section shall be designed and evaluated in accordance with the
263 recommendations of a report that was produced pursuant to special act
264 24-8. Not later than January 1, 2027, and annually thereafter until the
265 pilot program concludes, the Commissioners of Social Services and
266 Early Childhood shall file a report, in accordance with the provisions of
267 section 11-4a of the general statutes, on the status of the pilot program
268 with the joint standing committees of the General Assembly having
269 cognizance of matters relating to education, human services, labor and
270 housing. The report shall include, but need not be limited to: (1) The
271 number of households enrolled in the pilot program, (2) transitional
272 assistance such households have received or will receive to avert
273 benefits cliffs, (3) the impact on the state economy of pilot program
274 enrollees retaining employment while in the pilot program, and (4)
275 resources needed to sustain the pilot program.
