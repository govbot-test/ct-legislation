35 (a) Any person may submit a petition to the Commissioner of Energy
36 and Environmental Protection for a safe, timely and effective fish
37 passage for any hydroelectric dam that is not subject to the jurisdiction
38 of the Federal Energy Regulatory Commission. Upon the submittal of
39 such a petition, the commissioner shall determine whether there is a
40 safe, timely and effective fish passage for migratory fish at all life stages
41 upstream and downstream of such dam and associated reservoir. For
42 purposes of this section, a fish passage is deemed safe, timely and
43 effective if it meets or exceeds the Connecticut River Migratory Fish
44 Restoration Cooperative's fish passage standards developed by the
45 Connecticut River Atlantic Salmon Commission.
46 (b) If the commissioner determines that there is not a safe, timely and
47 effective fish passage, the commissioner shall prescribe, by order, issued
48 to the person, firm, corporation or municipality, or political subdivision
49 thereof, that owns or controls such dam, the conditions to ensure such a
50 safe, timely and effective fish passage both upstream of such reservoir
51 and downstream of such dam. Such conditions may include, but shall
52 not be limited to, requirements to (1) construct, rebuild and operate
