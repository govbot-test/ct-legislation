General Assembly Raised Bill No. 396
February Session, 2026 LCO No. 2037
Referred to Committee on HIGHER EDUCATION AND
EMPLOYMENT ADVANCEMENT
Introduced by:
(HED)
AN ACT PERMITTING NINETY-CREDIT DEGREE PROGRAMS AT
INSTITUTIONS OF HIGHER EDUCATION IN THE STATE.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General
Assembly convened:
1 Section 1. Section 10a-34 of the general statutes is amended by adding
2 subsection (m) as follows (Effective July 1, 2026):
3 (NEW) (m) The Office of Higher Education shall permit institutions
4 of higher education in the state to submit an application for a new
5 degree program through which a degree may be earned after the
6 completion of not fewer than ninety credit hours, provided such
7 program has accreditation recognized by the Secretary of the United
8 States Department of Education. The office shall conduct a review of
9 each application submitted under this subsection pursuant to the
10 provisions of this section, including, but not limited to, a focused or on-
11 site review, when necessary.
This act shall take effect as follows and shall amend the following
sections:
