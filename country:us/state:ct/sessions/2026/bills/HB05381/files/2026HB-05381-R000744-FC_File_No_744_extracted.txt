Explanation
The bill requires the Department of Consumer Protection (DCP) to
oversee the funeral service guaranty account (FSGA) and make grants
to eligible applicants resulting in a cost to the state. To meet the
requirements of the bill, DCP will have to hire a paralegal specialist for
a salary and other expenses cost of $76,750 in FY 282, along with
associated fringe benefit costs of $31,260 in FY 28. The new employee is
needed to review applications, make payments from the account, and
answer applicant questions.

OFA Bill Analysis
sHB 5381 (File 637, as amended by House "A")
AN ACT ESTABLISHING AN ACCOUNT TO COMPENSATE VICTIMS
OF UNLAWFUL FUNERAL SERVICE PRACTICES.
SUMMARY:
The bill makes various changes regarding funeral services laws,
described below.
*House Amendment "A" (1) adds to the requirements the Department
of Consumer Protection (DCP) must follow when administering the
funeral service guaranty account, (2) requires DCP to adopt regulations,
(3) alters the requirements of funeral service contracts, and (4) creates a
working group to study prepaid funeral service contracts.
EFFECTIVE DATE: Sections 1 and 3 are effective from passage,
section 2 is effective July 1, 2026.
Funeral Service Guaranty Account
The bill establishes a separate, non-lapsing account, called the
"funeral service guaranty account." The account must contain (1) any
amounts appropriated or made available by the state, (2) any monies
required to be deposited in the account, and (3) gifts, grants, donations,
or bequests. Investment earnings shall become part of the assets of the
account and the State Treasurer is required to administer the account.
The bill allows DCP to deduct and retain up to two percent of the
balance in the account for costs incurred by the department for
administering the account.
Beginning July 1, 2027, DCP is required to use the funds in the
account to provide restitution to eligible persons who were victims of

fraudulent or unlawful practices when purchasing funeral services.
DCP cannot provide restitution that exceeds the balance in the account.
An eligible person may make a claim from the account by submitting
the following documents to DCP:
1. A copy of the original funeral service contract,
2. Proof of any funeral services provided under the contract,
3. Proof that the person is a beneficiary or purchaser of a
contract, the estate of a beneficiary, or a legally authorized
agent of a beneficiary,
4. Proof of any payment for funeral or burial services not
provided in the contract.
DCP may consider a court order entered against the provider of the
funeral service contract with the eligible person but will not require the
document to be submitted.
Payments from the Funeral Service Guaranty Account
DCP must follow these requirements when dispersing funds from the
account:
1. No payment over $10,000 will be made from the account for
any one contract.
2. To preserve the integrity of the account DCP may order
payment in an amount less than the eligible person's actual
loss or damages or less than the restitution awarded.
3. If the account has insufficient funds to satisfy a claim, DCP
shall satisfy any unpaid claim amounts once sufficient funds
have been deposited into the account, in the order that the
claims or portions of claims were determined.
4. Once an eligible person has been paid from the account, DCP

Regulations
The bill requires DCP to adopt regulations concerning requirements
for obtaining restitution for eligible persons no later that April 1, 2028
or ninety days after the issued report from the working group. DCP will
also adopt policies and procedures to implement the provisions of the
bill not later than June 1, 2027.
Funeral Services Contracts
The bill makes the following changes to funeral services contracts:
1. That the notice from the escrow agent acknowledging receipt
of the initial deposit be in bold type and state the amount of
the deposit.
2. The contract is required to include a statement signed by the
purchaser saying that prior to executing the contract the
purchaser received: (1) the DCP fact sheet on funeral service
contracts and (2) had time to review the fact sheet.
Prepaid Funeral Service Contracts Working Group
The bill creates a working group to study issues related to prepaid
funeral service contracts, the creation of consumer protections for these
contracts, and the establishment and implementation of the guaranty
fund for these consumers.
The working group consists of twelve members appointed by various
