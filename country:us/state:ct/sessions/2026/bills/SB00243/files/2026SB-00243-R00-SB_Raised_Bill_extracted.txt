AN ACT REQUIRING A STUDY OF THE DESIGN OF RESIDENTIAL
ELECTRIC CUSTOMER BILLS.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General
Assembly convened:
1 Section 1. (Effective October 1, 2026) The chairperson of the Public
2 Utilities Regulatory Authority shall conduct a study that analyzes the
3 depiction of the public benefits charge on the electric bill for customers
4 of each electric distribution company. Such study shall consider
5 whether the public benefits charge as depicted on such bills adequately
6 informs customers of the components of such charge. If the chairperson
7 finds that the depiction of the public benefits charge does not contain
8 adequate information, the chairperson shall provide detailed
9 recommendations for additional information to increase a customer's
10 understanding of such charge and recommendations on how customer
11 electric bills may be redesigned, including the creation of new electric
12 bill cost categories, to increase transparency concerning the costs and
13 benefits of programs funded through charges on a customer's electric
14 bill. In developing any recommendations concerning the redesign of
15 customer electric bills, the chairperson shall solicit written comment
16 from each electric distribution company. Not later than January 1, 2027,
