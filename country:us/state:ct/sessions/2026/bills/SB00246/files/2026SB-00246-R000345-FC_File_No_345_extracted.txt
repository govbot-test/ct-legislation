AN ACT REQUIRING A STUDY OF NATURAL GAS RATES FOR
COMMERCIAL AND AGRICULTURAL CUSTOMERS.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General
Assembly convened:
1 Section 1. (Effective October 1, 2026) The Commissioner of Energy and
2 Environmental Protection shall conduct a study of natural gas rates,
3 including, but not limited to, any applicable demand charges, for
4 commercial and agricultural natural gas customers who have
5 intermittent peak monthly demand to determine if a significant number
6 of such customers are charged rates that do not fairly reflect such
7 customers' use due to such demand charges. If the commissioner
8 determines that such demand charges have the effect of unfairly
9 increasing rates for such customers, the commissioner shall provide
10 detailed recommendations concerning how such charges may be
11 recalculated or otherwise applied to such customers. Not later than
12 January 15, 2027, the commissioner shall submit a report, in accordance
13 with the provisions of section 11-4a of the general statutes, that contains
14 such recommendations to the joint standing committee of the General

Explanation
The bill requires the Department of Energy and Environmental
Protection (DEEP) to conduct a study of natural gas rates. This will not
result in a fiscal impact as DEEP has the staff and expertise necessary to
complete the study.
