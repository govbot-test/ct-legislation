58 Sec. 3. (NEW) (Effective October 1, 2026) (a) A direct-to-consumer
59 genetic testing company shall:
60 (1) At all times transact its business and conduct its affairs in a
61 manner that is consistent with a consumer's rights under section 2 of this
62 act;
63 (2) Prior to accepting any biological sample, genetic data or payment
64 from a consumer, disclose to the consumer the company's policies and
65 procedures concerning the collection, use and disclosure of genetic data;
66 (3) Display, in a prominent and publicly accessible location on the
67 company's Internet web site, a privacy notice disclosing such company's
68 policies and procedures concerning the collection, use, access,
69 disclosure, transfer, security, retention and deletion of a consumer's
70 data and the consumer's consent thereto;
71 (4) Prior to collecting, using or disclosing a consumer's genetic data,
72 obtain the consumer's express consent for such collection, use or
73 disclosure after disclosing to such consumer (A) the company's policies
74 and procedures concerning use of the genetic data such company
75 collects from consumers, (B) the identity of each person who may access
76 the results of genetic testing performed by the company, including, but
77 not limited to, any vendor or service provider for such company who
78 may access such results, and (C) the manner in which the company may
79 disclose such consumer's genetic data;
