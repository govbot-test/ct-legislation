Explanation
The bill results in annual revenue gain of less than $1,000 to the
Department of Motor Vehicles by authorizing the agency to issue
colored light permits to organ transport vehicles. Under current law, an
annual permit fee of $20 generally applies for use of colored and flashing
lights.
The Out Years
The annualized ongoing fiscal impact identified above would
continue into the future subject to the number of permits issued.

OLR Bill Analysis
SB 407
AN ACT AUTHORIZING THE COMMISSIONER OF MOTOR
VEHICLES TO ISSUE PERMITS ALLOWING ORGAN TRANSPORT
VEHICLES TO USE CERTAIN LIGHTS.
SUMMARY
By law, a permit is generally required to use colored or flashing lights
on motor vehicles or equipment. This bill allows the Department of
Motor Vehicles (DMV) commissioner to issue permits for organ
transport vehicles to use any combination of steady or flashing blue, red,
yellow, or white lights, unless specifically restricted for use by police
officers or DMV inspectors. By law, using colored or flashing lights
unauthorized is an infraction.
Under the bill, an organ transport vehicle is any vehicle driven by an
employee of a licensed organ procurement organization that is being
used for transporting parts (an organ, eye, or human tissue, but not a
whole human body).
EFFECTIVE DATE: October 1, 2026
BACKGROUND
Related Bill
HB 5460, favorably reported by the Public Safety and Security
Committee, among other things, removes the ban on using steady red,
blue, or red and blue lights that are externally visible from the vehicle’s
front.
