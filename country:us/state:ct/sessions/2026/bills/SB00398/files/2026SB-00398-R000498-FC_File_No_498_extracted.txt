Explanation
The bill, which replaces the current $110 per diem payment for the
part-time members of the Board of Pardons and Paroles with an amount
determined by the Department of Administrative Services (DAS),
results in a cost to the Department of Correction of up to $192,000 in FY
27 and FY 28, to the extent that these payments are required.
The new per diem rate is estimated to be about $430 and each of the
five part-time board members are anticipated to work up to 120 days
per year. However, these positions are currently vacant. Each position
that is filled is estimated to incur an additional cost of up to $38,400
annually.1
The Out Years
The annualized ongoing fiscal impact identified above would
continue into the future subject to the per diem rate set by DAS.
1 Under current law, each of the part-time positions would cost up to $13,200 annually

OLR Bill Analysis
SB 398
AN ACT CONCERNING THE BOARD OF PARDONS AND PAROLES.
SUMMARY
This bill replaces the current $110 per diem payment for part-time
members of the Board of Pardons and Paroles with an amount, subject
to available appropriations, that the Department of Administrative
Services (DAS) commissioner determines. The commissioner’s set rate
is subject to the Office of Policy and Management (OPM) secretary’s
approval. As under existing law, part-time board members must also be
reimbursed for necessary expenses.
By law, the board includes 10 full-time members and up to five part-
time members. The DAS commissioner sets the salary of full-time
members, subject to the OPM secretary’s approval.
