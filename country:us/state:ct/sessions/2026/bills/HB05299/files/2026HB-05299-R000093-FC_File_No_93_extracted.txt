Explanation
The bill authorizes licensed registered nurses to delegate the
administration of certain medications to certain qualified assisted living
aides, and the Department of Public Health to establish regulations for
the administration of medications, resulting in no fiscal impact to the
state. DPH has the necessary expertise to develop the regulations.

OLR Bill Analysis
sHB 5299
AN ACT ALLOWING TRAINED ASSISTED LIVING AIDES TO
ADMINISTER MEDICATION TO ASSISTED LIVING RESIDENTS.
SUMMARY
This bill authorizes licensed registered nurses to delegate the
administration of certain medications to assisted living aides who are
employed by an assisted living services agency (ALSA) and trained in
medication administration, as described below. Under current law, only
an ALSA’s licensed nursing personnel can administer medications to
residents under a prescribing practitioner’s orders, and assisted living
aides may only supervise residents self-administering medications.
The bill requires the Department of Public Health commissioner to
adopt related regulations. An assisted living aide must complete a
training as required by these regulations to be qualified to administer
medications, including any controlled substances specified in the
regulations, but not medications that are injected.
Under the bill, to delegate medication administration to these trained
aides, a licensed registered nurse must be employed by an ALSA and
authorized by the agency to do so. The bill also provides certain
protections for these nurses. Specifically, (1) the State Board of
Examiners for Nursing cannot discipline them solely for delegating
medication administration as the bill allows and (2) they are generally
immune from civil liability if an aide’s actions, or failure to act, result in
personal injuries or financial loss. However, under the bill, nurses are
not immune if their actions, or failures to act, are found to constitute
gross negligence or willful or wanton misconduct.
EFFECTIVE DATE: October 1, 2026
