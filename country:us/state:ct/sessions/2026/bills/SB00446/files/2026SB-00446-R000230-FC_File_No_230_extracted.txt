AN ACT REQUIRING THE ADVISORY COUNCIL ON
INTERGOVERNMENTAL RELATIONS TO CONDUCT A STUDY
CONCERNING THE FEASIBILITY OF AUTHORIZING MUNICIPALITIES
THAT HAVE ENTERED INTO REVENUE SHARING AGREEMENTS TO
ADOPT DIFFERENTIAL MILL RATES.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General
Assembly convened:
1 Section 1. (Effective from passage) The Connecticut Advisory
2 Commission on Intergovernmental Relations established pursuant to
3 section 2-79a of the general statutes shall conduct a study concerning
4 the feasibility of authorizing municipalities that have entered into
5 revenue sharing agreements pursuant to section 7-148bb of the general
6 statutes to adopt differential mill rates applicable to taxable real
7 property for which the tax revenues are shared pursuant to such
8 agreements. Not later than January 1, 2027, the commission shall submit
9 a report, in accordance with the provisions of section 11-4a of the general
10 statutes, to the joint standing committee of the General Assembly
11 having cognizance of matters relating to planning and development.

Explanation
The bill requires the Connecticut Advisory Commission on
Intergovernmental Relations within the Office of Policy and
Management (OPM) to (1) conduct a study regarding the feasibility of
setting different mill rates for municipalities that have entered into
revenue sharing agreements, and (2) submit a report by January 1, 2027.
This results in a one-time cost of $50,000 to OPM in FY 27 to conduct the
study and submit it by the required deadline.

None.
