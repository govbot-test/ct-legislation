AN ACT CONCERNING PATIENT PROTECTIONS AND STANDARDS
OF CARE AT CONNECTICUT VALLEY HOSPITAL.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General
Assembly convened:
1 Section 1. Section 17a-471a of the general statutes is repealed and the
2 following is substituted in lieu thereof (Effective October 1, 2026):
3 (a) The Commissioner of Mental Health and Addiction Services, in
4 consultation and coordination with the advisory council established
5 under subsection (b) of this section, shall develop policies and set
6 standards [related] relating to clients residing on the Connecticut Valley
7 Hospital campus and to the discharge of such clients from the hospital
8 into the adjacent community, and persons undergoing psychiatric
9 evaluation at Connecticut Valley Hospital. Any such policies and
10 standards shall (1) ensure that no discharge of any client admitted to
11 Whiting Forensic Hospital under commitment by the Superior Court or
12 transfer from the Department of Correction shall take place without full
13 compliance with sections 17a-511 to 17a-524, inclusive, 17a-566 to 17a-
14 575, inclusive, 17a-580 to 17a-603, inclusive, and 54-56d, and (2) require
15 persons undergoing psychiatric evaluation at Connecticut Valley

17 (b) There is established a Connecticut Valley Hospital Advisory
18 Council that shall advise the Commissioner of Mental Health and
19 Addiction Services on policies concerning, but not limited to, building

28 and after January 1, 2024, at least two members appointed by the
29 Commissioner of Mental Health and Addiction Services shall have lived
30 experience with a behavioral health disorder.
This act shall take effect as follows and shall amend the following
sections:
Section 1 October 1, 2026 17a-471a
Statement of Purpose:
To require the Commissioner of Mental Health and Addiction Services
to keep persons undergoing psychiatric evaluation at Connecticut
Valley Hospital separated according to biological sex at all times.
