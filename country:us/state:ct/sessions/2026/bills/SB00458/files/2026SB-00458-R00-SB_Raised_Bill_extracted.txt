AN ACT DESIGNATING VARIOUS DAYS AND MONTHS.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General
Assembly convened:
1 Section 1. Subdivision (9) of subsection (a) of section 10-29a of the
2 2026 supplement to the general statutes is repealed and the following is
3 substituted in lieu thereof (Effective from passage):
4 (9) The Governor shall proclaim the last Friday in September in each
5 year to be [Indian] Indigenous Peoples' Day, which day shall be suitably
6 observed in the public schools of the state as a day of commemoration
7 of [American Indians] Native Americans and their contribution to
8 American life and civilization.
9 Sec. 2. Subdivision (133) of subsection (a) of section 10-29a of the 2026
10 supplement to the general statutes is repealed and the following is
11 substituted in lieu thereof (Effective from passage):
12 (133) The Governor shall proclaim the third Sunday after the third
13 Saturday of September of each year as Lobster Roll Day. Suitable
14 exercises may be held in the State Capitol and elsewhere as the

16 Sec. 3. Subsection (a) of section 10-29a of the 2026 supplement to the
17 general statutes is amended by adding subdivisions (139) to (148),
18 inclusive, as follows (Effective from passage):
19 (NEW) (139) The Governor shall proclaim January eleventh of each
20 year as Hartford Whalers Day, in honor of the first Whalers game played
21 in the city of Hartford. Suitable exercises may be held in the State Capitol
22 and elsewhere as the Governor designates for the observance of the day.
23 (NEW) (140) The Governor shall proclaim February third of each year
24 as Topical Steroid Withdrawal Awareness Day, to raise public
25 awareness of this adverse reaction characterized by intense burning,
26 red, inflamed skin and peeling that occurs after stopping long-term or
27 high-potency topical steroid creams. Suitable exercises may be held in
28 the State Capitol and elsewhere as the Governor designates for the
29 observance of the day.
30 (NEW) (141) The Governor shall proclaim March fourth of each year
31 as 6888th Central Postal Directory Battalion Day, to recognize the only
32 African-American all-female unit sent overseas during World War II to
33 process millions of pieces of backlogged mail for troops in the European
34 theater. Suitable exercises may be held in the State Capitol and
35 elsewhere as the Governor designates for the observance of the day.
36 (NEW) (142) The Governor shall proclaim March eighth of each year
37 as Henry Breault Day, to remember the late Medal of Honor recipient
38 from Putnam who was the first submariner to be presented the Medal
39 of Honor on March 8, 1924, for his actions aboard a United States
40 submarine that was sinking, when he returned to the torpedo room to
41 the rescue of a shipmate and remained trapped in the submarine for
42 thirty-one hours. Suitable exercises may be held in the State Capitol and
43 elsewhere as the Governor designates for the observance of the day.
44 (NEW) (143) The Governor shall proclaim the first day of the Islamic

45 month of Shawwal, the tenth month of the Islamic lunar calendar of each
46 year, as Eid al-Fitr, to mark the end of Ramadan. Suitable exercises may
47 be held in the State Capitol and elsewhere as the Governor designates

49 (NEW) (144) The Governor shall proclaim the month of May of each
50 year as Progressive Supranuclear Palsy Awareness Month, to raise
51 public awareness of the associated presentation and available
52 treatments for this rare, progressive neurodegenerative disorder
53 affecting movement, balance, vision and speech due to a buildup of
54 proteins in the brain. Suitable exercises may be held in the State Capitol
55 and elsewhere as the Governor designates for the observance of the

57 (NEW) (145) The Governor shall proclaim July seventeenth of each
58 year as Hiram Bingham IV Day, to remember the late Connecticut
59 resident who was the Vice Consul in charge of visas at the United States
60 Consulate in Marseilles, France, from 1940 to 1941, and who issued
61 thousands of visas to refugees and was instrumental in assisting them
62 in escaping to the United States, at great personal peril. Suitable
63 exercises may be held in the State Capitol and elsewhere as the
64 Governor designates for the observance of the day.
65 (NEW) (146) The Governor shall proclaim the month of September of
66 each year as Ovarian Cancer Awareness Month, to raise public
67 awareness of the associated presentation and available treatments for
68 ovarian cancer. Suitable exercises may be held in the State Capitol and
69 elsewhere as the Governor designates for the observance of the month.
70 (NEW) (147) The Governor shall proclaim September twenty-eighth
71 of each year as Good Neighbor Day, to combat social isolation and
72 strengthen community bonds, encourage people to get to know their
73 neighbors, practice acts of kindness and build safer, more connected
74 neighborhoods. Suitable exercises may be held in the State Capitol and
75 elsewhere as the Governor designates for the observance of the day.
