AN ACT CONCERNING THE AVAILABILITY OF BREAST CANCER
SCREENING, DIAGNOSIS AND TREATMENT SERVICES FOR WOMEN
COMMITTED TO THE CUSTODY OF THE DEPARTMENT OF
CORRECTION.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General
Assembly convened:
1 Section 1. (NEW) (Effective July 1, 2026) The Commissioner of
2 Correction, when providing medical care for the screening, diagnosis
3 and treatment of breast cancer to any woman committed to the custody
4 of the Department of Correction, may arrange for such screening,
5 diagnostic and treatment services to be provided by a health care
6 institution, licensed in accordance with the provisions of chapter 368v
7 of the general statutes, that is in closer proximity to the correctional
8 facility than The University of Connecticut Health Center. In the event
9 the commissioner is unable to provide a required diagnostic and
10 screening mammogram, as defined in section 38a-503 of the general
11 statutes, to a woman committed to the custody of the commissioner on-
12 site at the correctional facility, the commissioner may then arrange for
13 the provision of such mammogram at a health care institution that is in
14 closer proximity to the correctional facility than The University of
