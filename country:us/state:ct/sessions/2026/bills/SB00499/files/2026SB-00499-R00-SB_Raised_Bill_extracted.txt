AN ACT CONCERNING MEDICAID RATE INCREASES.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General
Assembly convened:
1 Section 1. (NEW) (Effective July 1, 2026) (a) As used in this section, (1)
2 "Medicaid rate study" means the study commissioned by the
3 Department of Social Services pursuant to section 1 of public act 23-186,
4 (2) "five-state rate benchmark" means the average of rates for the same
5 health care services in Maine, Massachusetts, New Jersey, New York
6 and Oregon, and (3) "Medicare Economic Index" means a measure of
7 inflation for physicians with respect to their practice costs and wage
8 levels as calculated by the Centers for Medicare and Medicaid Services.
9 (b) Within available appropriations, the Commissioner of Social
10 Services shall phase in increases to Medicaid provider rates in
11 accordance with the Medicaid rate study. The commissioner shall phase
12 in the rate increases commencing on July 1, 2026, such that by June 30,
13 2029, all such rates equal (1) not less than seventy-five per cent of the
14 most recent Medicare rates for the same health care services, or (2) for
15 such services with no corresponding Medicare rates, a percentage of the
16 five-state benchmark that results in an equivalent rate increase.

17 (c) On and after June 30, 2029, the commissioner shall adjust such
18 rates every year (1) to not less than seventy-five per cent of the most
19 recent Medicare rates for the same health care services, (2) to an
20 equivalent percentage of the five-state rate benchmark for such services
21 with no corresponding Medicare rates, or (3) by increasing such rates by
22 any percentage increase in the Medicare Economic Index.
23 (d) Any review or rebasing of Medicaid rates shall include those rates
24 (1) required to be studied pursuant to public act 23-186; and (2) with no
25 corresponding (A) Medicare rate for the same health care service, or (B)
26 average benchmark rate included in the Medicaid rate study. If any one
27 state within the five-state benchmark group has a corresponding rate for
28 the same or substantially similar health care service, such rate shall be
29 used for comparison in such review.
30 (e) The commissioner shall streamline and consolidate existing fee
31 schedules used for provider or service reimbursement so that every
32 provider is reimbursed using the same fee schedule. In streamlining and
33 consolidating existing fee schedules, the commissioner shall
34 incorporate, to the extent applicable, the most recent Medicare fee
35 schedule for services covered by Medicare as well as Medicaid.
36 Sec. 2. (NEW) (Effective July 1, 2026) (a) The Council on Medical
37 Assistance Program Oversight, established pursuant to section 17b-28
38 of the general statutes, shall develop and implement an ongoing
39 systemic review of Medicaid provider reimbursement rates to ensure
40 rates are adequate to sustain a sufficient provider pool to provide
41 Medicaid member access to high-quality care.
42 (b) Not later than January 15, 2027, and annually thereafter, the
43 council shall file a report, in accordance with the provisions of section
44 11-4a of the general statutes, with the joint standing committees of the
45 General Assembly having cognizance of matters relating to
46 appropriations and the budgets of state agencies and human services.
47 The report shall include the council's recommendations on necessary
