Explanation
The bill results in a cost to the Department of Social Services (DSS)
associated with creating a state-funded respite program to support
caregivers of individuals with autism spectrum disorder who are
enrolled in the Medicaid Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD) waiver
program. The cost will depend on funds appropriated to the program
and additional staff and resources needed to operate the program.
For reference, the most recent Autism Waiver Waitlist report (April
2025) reported that there were 2,249 individuals on the waitlist, 217
individuals actively receiving services, and 140 individuals assigned a
case manager and moving through the enrollment process.
The Out Years
The annualized ongoing fiscal impact identified above would
continue into the future subject to inflation.

SUMMARY
This bill requires the Department of Social Services commissioner,
within available appropriations, to establish a state-funded caregiver
respite program for parents of adult children enrolled in the Medicaid
Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD) waiver program.
By law, the ASD waiver program provides home- and community-
based services to people with ASD who do not have an intellectual
disability (an intelligence quotient score of at least 70). To qualify, a
person must have substantial limitations in two or more specified major
life activities that were diagnosed before age 22 and are expected to
continue indefinitely.
