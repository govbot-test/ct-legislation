Explanation
The resolution places a proposed amendment on either the
November 2026 or November 2028 ballot to authorize eligible
Connecticut residents who are at least 16 to register to vote and
participate as electors once they reach the age of 18, resulting in a cost
to the Secretary of the State (SOTS) of $25,000 in either FY 27 or FY 29.
The timing of the cost is dependent on when the requirements for the
resolution's passage are met. The resolution must pass by three-quarters
of the General Assembly for the cost to be incurred in FY 27. If the
requisite votes are not met, but the resolution is approved by a majority
in that session and the next, then the cost to SOTS will shift to FY 29.
The cost to SOTS for placing the proposed amendment on the ballot
includes printing and postage for distributing the amendment to
municipalities for inclusion.
The Out Years
The SOTS cost of placing the amendment on the ballot may occur in
FY 29, as described above.

SUMMARY
This resolution proposes an amendment to the state constitution to
allow 16- and 17-year-old citizens to apply for admission as electors
(voters), with electoral privileges attaching at age 18 if the person
applies for admission under state law and is otherwise qualified.
Currently, under the state constitution, a citizen may apply for
admission as an elector if he or she will turn 18 on or before the day of
a regular election, with privileges attaching at age 18. By law and under
the state constitution, a 17-year-old who has applied and qualified for
admission as an elector, and who will turn age 18 on or before the day
of a regular election, may vote in the primary for that election. A
“regular election” is a municipal or state election.
The ballot designation to be used when the amendment is presented
at the general election is: “Shall the Constitution of the State be amended
to allow any citizen who has attained the age of sixteen years to
preregister to be an elector, for the purpose of becoming an elector on
such citizen’s eighteenth birthday?”
EFFECTIVE DATE: If the resolution passes by at least three-fourths
of the membership of each house of the General Assembly, it will be
placed on the 2026 general election ballot. If it passes by a majority of
the membership of each house but less than three-fourths, it will be
referred to the 2027 session of the legislature. If it passes in that session
by a majority of each house, it will appear on the 2028 general election
