AN ACT CONCERNING THE TESTING OF SOILS AT CERTAIN SOLAR
FACILITIES.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General
Assembly convened:
1 Section 1. (NEW) (Effective October 1, 2026) The Commissioner of
2 Energy and Environmental Protection shall develop a program for the
3 biennial testing of soil for contaminants, including, but not limited to,
4 degraded heavy metals and toxic substances such as cadmium, lead,
5 arsenic and zinc at utility-scale solar arrays in the state. Such program
6 shall include, but not be limited to: (1) Disclosure of soil test results to
7 the owner of the subject property, (2) provision for the remediation of
8 any such contamination, including any attendant groundwater
9 remediation, and recovery of associated costs from the applicable
10 responsible parties, and (3) appropriate public notification, including
11 any requisite land record recording, of such test results and effects of
12 such remediation such as any land use restriction or other limit on the
13 utilization of such property. The commissioner shall implement such
14 program not later than October 1, 2027, and may adopt regulations, in
15 accordance with the provisions of chapter 54 of the general statutes, to
16 implement the provisions of this section. Such regulations may provide
