AN ACT CONCERNING STERILE CULTIVARS.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General
Assembly convened:
1 Section 1. (NEW) (Effective from passage) Notwithstanding the
2 provisions of chapter 446i of the general statutes, the sale, distribution
3 or offering for sale of a sterile cultivar of a plant species that is otherwise
4 prohibited from such sale, distribution or offering for sale shall be
5 permitted, provided the Commissioner of Energy and Environmental
6 Protection, in consultation with the director of the Connecticut
7 Agricultural Experiment Station, determines that such cultivar is
8 incapable of reproducing or spreading under the environmental
9 conditions that are typical in the state. The commissioner may establish
10 standards for determining such sterility and may revoke approval if a
11 cultivar is found to pose an ecological risk. Nothing in this section shall
12 be construed to authorize the sale of nonsterile forms of any such
13 prohibited plant species.

OLR Bill Analysis
sHB 5521
AN ACT CONCERNING STERILE CULTIVARS.
SUMMARY
This bill allows the sale and distribution of sterile cultivars (plants
specifically bred or engineered to produce few or no viable seeds) of
species that are otherwise prohibited if the Department of Energy and
Environmental Protection (DEEP) commissioner, in consultation with
the Connecticut Agriculture Experiment Station director, determines
the cultivar is incapable of reproducing or spreading under
Connecticut’s typical environmental conditions. The bill allows the
commissioner to (1) set standards for determining sterility and (2)
revoke approval if she determines a cultivar is an ecological risk.
EFFECTIVE DATE: Upon passage
BACKGROUND
Invasive Plants
By law, Connecticut bans the import, sale, and cultivation of several
invasive plant species to protect native ecosystems (CGS § 22a-381d).
The state’s Invasive Plants Council, among other things, publishes a list
of invasive or potentially invasive plants, researches and makes
recommendations on the control of invasive plants, and educates the
public on related issues (CGS § 22a-381a).
