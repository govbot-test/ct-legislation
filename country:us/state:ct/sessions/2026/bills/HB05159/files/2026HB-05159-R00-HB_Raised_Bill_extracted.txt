AN ACT FUNDING JOURNALISM FELLOWSHIPS FOR GRADUATES
OF PUBLIC INSTITUTIONS OF HIGHER EDUCATION.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General
Assembly convened:
1 Section 1. (NEW) (Effective July 1, 2026) (a) As used in this section,
2 "local news organization" means a news organization, as defined in
3 section 54-33i of the general statutes, that is located in the state.
4 (b) For the fiscal year ending June 30, 2027, and each fiscal year
5 thereafter, the Office of Higher Education shall establish a journalism
6 fellowship program to fund not fewer than twelve journalism
7 fellowships at one or more local news organizations. The office shall
8 specify the (1) minimum requirements for a fellowship funded under
9 such program, including, but not limited to, opportunities for (A) a
10 mentorship from experienced journalists and editors, (B) direct work
11 experience, and (C) professional networking, and (2) eligibility
12 requirements for individuals to qualify for such journalism fellowships,
13 including, but not limited to, (A) graduating from a journalism degree
14 program at a public institution of higher education in the state, and (B)
