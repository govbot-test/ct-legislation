February Session, 2026 LCO No. 2621
Referred to Committee on HIGHER EDUCATION AND
EMPLOYMENT ADVANCEMENT
Introduced by:
(HED)
AN ACT CONCERNING DUAL ENROLLMENT PROGRAMS.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General
Assembly convened:
1 Section 1. Section 10-221w of the 2026 supplement to the general
2 statutes is repealed and the following is substituted in lieu thereof
3 (Effective July 1, 2026):
4 (a) As used in this section:
5 (1) "Advanced course or program" means an honors class, advanced
6 placement class, International Baccalaureate program, Cambridge
7 International program, dual enrollment, dual credit, early college or any
8 other advanced or accelerated course or program offered by a local or
9 regional board of education in grades nine to twelve, inclusive; and
10 (2) "Prior academic performance" means the course or courses that a
11 student has taken, the grades received for such course or courses and a
12 student's grade point average.
13 (b) Not later than July 1, 2022, each local and regional board of
LCO No. 2621 1 of 4
Raised Bill No. 427
14 education shall adopt a policy, or revise an existing policy, concerning
15 the eligibility criteria for student enrollment in an advanced course or
16 program. Such policy shall provide for multiple methods by which a
17 student may satisfy the eligibility criteria for enrollment in an advanced
18 course or program, including, but not limited to, recommendations
19 from teachers, administrators, school counselors or other school
20 personnel. Such eligibility criteria shall not be based exclusively on a
21 student's prior academic performance and any use of a student's prior
22 academic performance shall rely on evidence-based indicators of how a
23 student will perform in an advanced course or program.
24 (c) Any policy adopted or revised and implemented under this
25 section shall be in accordance with guidance provided by the
26 Department of Education.
27 (d) (1) Not later than July 1, 2026, the Commissioner of Education
28 shall, in partnership with the constituent units of the state system of
29 higher education and independent institutions of higher education, as
30 defined in section 10a-173, develop a model agreement between
31 secondary schools and postsecondary institutions for the provision of
32 dual enrollment courses and concurrent enrollment courses, as such
33 terms are defined in section 10a-35d, and postsecondary credit courses
34 to students in grades nine to twelve, inclusive.
35 (2) Not later than January 1, 2027, the Commissioner of Education
36 shall appoint a dual enrollment program coordinator to track the
37 establishment of such programs throughout the state and student
38 outcomes from such programs, including, but not limited to, completion
39 rates and average grades, by school district.
40 (e) For the fiscal year ending June 30, 2027, and each fiscal year
41 thereafter, the Commissioner of Education shall, within available
42 appropriations, establish a fee-waiver grant program to expand
43 opportunities for high-need high school students to access advanced
44 courses or programs. A local or regional board of education may apply,
LCO No. 2621 2 of 4
Raised Bill No. 427
45 in a form and manner prescribed by the Commissioner of Education, for
46 reimbursement for any fees charged to such board for any high-need
47 student who enrolls in an advanced course or program.
48 (f) For the fiscal year ending June 30, 2027, and each fiscal year
49 thereafter, the Commissioner of Education may, within available
50 appropriations, pay up to five hundred thousand dollars in a fiscal year
51 to the State Education Resource Center for programming that provides
52 direct support to local and regional boards of education in the
53 articulation and expansion of dual credit courses. In expending such
54 funds under this subsection, the State Education Resource Center shall
55 give priority to providing funds to alliance districts.
56 (g) For the fiscal year ending June 30, 2027, and each fiscal year
57 thereafter, the Commissioner of Education shall, within available
58 appropriations, establish a need-based dual enrollment scholarship
59 program to reimburse parents of students enrolled in a dual enrollment
60 program who incurred an enrollment or tuition cost for such program
61 and have financial need, as determined by the commissioner. The
62 commissioner shall post the eligibility criteria and application form for
63 such scholarship in a conspicuous place on the Department of
64 Education's Internet web site and the dual enrollment program
65 coordinator shall notify each local and regional board of education
66 about the dual enrollment scholarship program.

Section 1 July 1, 2026 10-221w
Statement of Purpose:
To require the Department of Education to appoint a dual enrollment
coordinator to track state-wide dual enrollment programs and to
provide scholarships to students required to pay tuition costs for dual
enrollment programs.
