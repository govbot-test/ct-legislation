Explanation
The bill results in a cost to the Department of Energy and
Environmental Protection (DEEP) of up to $2 million, by December 1,
2028, associated with the development of an online pesticide sales and
use database. It is estimated, based on similar databases DEEP has
developed, that the costs would include, up to $1 million for a system
development contractor, $700,000 for licensing, hosting and
construction support, and $300,000 to incorporate previously recorded
data that is required under the bill. The bill appropriates $100,000 to
DEEP in FY 27 to develop and implement the database.
The Out Years
The outyear costs are estimated to be approximately $100,000
annually and include ongoing licensing and data maintenance fees.

OLR Bill Analysis
HB 5155
AN ACT CONCERNING PESTICIDE REPORTING MODERNIZATION.
SUMMARY
This bill (1) requires the Department of Energy and Environmental
Protection (DEEP) to have an online pesticide sales and use database
available by December 1, 2028, and (2) appropriates $100,000 to DEEP
in FY 27 to develop and implement it.
Under the bill, DEEP’s database must enable the public and
government entities to search and track data on pesticides sold or used
in the state. It must (1) have the information existing law requires DEEP
to receive on pesticide sales and use, such as the locations and quantities
of certain pesticide applications, and (2) be able to include information
that DEEP tracked or collected before July 1, 2026.
The bill also requires DEEP to collaborate with the Department of
Agriculture to collect information on pesticide-treated seeds sales in the
state and have it available in the database.
