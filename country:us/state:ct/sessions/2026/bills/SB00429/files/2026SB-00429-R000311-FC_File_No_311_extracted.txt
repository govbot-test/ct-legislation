OLR Bill Analysis
SB 429
AN ACT CONCERNING MEDICAID REIMBURSEMENT FOR
EMERGENCY MEDICAL TRANSPORTATION.
SUMMARY
This bill sets requirements for how the Department of Social Services
(DSS) calculates mileage to reimburse ambulance services under the
state medical assistance program (which includes Medicaid).
Specifically, the bill requires the DSS commissioner to calculate out-of-
district mileage for these services based on an ambulance’s distance
traveled with the patient onboard from the point of patient pickup to
the destination, rounded to the nearest one-tenth of a mile.
The bill allows an ambulance service to use any reasonable and
verifiable method to document mileage, including global positioning
system (GPS) data, navigation or dispatch computer systems, or
commercially available mapping routing programs. Under the bill, the
DSS commissioner must require ambulance service providers to
maintain mileage documentation in the patient care record, including a
trip report with the patient’s name, street address, pickup point, and
destination.
