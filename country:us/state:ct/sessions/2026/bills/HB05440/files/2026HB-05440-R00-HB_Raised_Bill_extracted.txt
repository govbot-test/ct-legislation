General Assembly Raised Bill No. 5440
February Session, 2026 LCO No. 2481
Referred to Committee on JUDICIARY
Introduced by:
(JUD)
AN ACT CONCERNING THE AWARD OF REASONABLE ATTORNEY'S
FEES BY THE COURT IN ENTRY AND DETAINER CASES.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General
Assembly convened:
1 Section 1. Subsection (a) of section 47a-45a of the general statutes is
2 repealed and the following is substituted in lieu thereof (Effective October
3 1, 2026):
4 (a) If it is found (1) that a forcible entry has been made into the land,
5 tenement or dwelling unit, or (2) that the same are detained with force
6 and strong hand, or (3) that damage has been caused to the premises or
7 damage to or removal of or detention of the personal property of the
8 possessor, or (4) that the party put out of possession would be required
9 to cause damage to the premises or commit a breach of the peace in
10 order to regain possession, as complained of, the judge shall render
11 judgment that the complainant be restored to, and reseized of, the
12 premises or that the personal property removed or detained be returned
13 to the complainant, and shall award a writ of restitution accordingly.
14 The complainant shall recover costs and reasonable attorney's fees from
15 the person complained of. Execution shall be granted against the person

17 Sec. 2. Section 47a-46 of the general statutes is repealed and the

Section 1 October 1, 2026 47a-45a(a)

Statement of Purpose:
To permit the court to award reasonable attorney's fees in entry and
detainer cases.
