AN ACT ESTABLISHING A CAREER PIPELINE PROGRAM FOR
AMERICAN SIGN LANGUAGE INTERPRETING AND INCREASING
STATE ASSISTANCE FOR PERSONS WHO ARE BLIND OR
VISUALLY IMPAIRED AND DEAF.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General
Assembly convened:
1 Section 1. (NEW) (Effective July 1, 2026) (a) The Commissioner of
2 Education shall establish a three-year pilot program to promote a career
3 pipeline for American Sign Language interpreting by providing not
4 more than five local or regional school districts with funding for related
5 high school courses eligible for college credit.
6 (b) The commissioner shall prescribe application requirements for the
7 pilot program and annual reporting requirements for school districts
8 selected for the program. A school district selected for the program shall
9 sign a memorandum of understanding with an accredited institution of
10 higher education to provide credit from such institution for program
11 courses.
12 (c) Instruction offered under the pilot program may include, but need
13 not be limited to, vocabulary development, grammar and syntax under

15 conversational proficiency and advanced communication skills

18 (d) For the duration of the pilot program, the commissioner shall file

27 appropriated to the state Department of Education from the General

29 established pursuant to section 1 of this act.
30 Sec. 3. Subsection (d) of section 17a-812 of the general statutes is
31 repealed and the following is substituted in lieu thereof (Effective July 1,
32 2026):
33 (d) The Commissioner of Aging and Disability Services may expend
34 up to [ten] fourteen thousand dollars per fiscal year per person twenty-
35 one years of age or older who is both blind or visually impaired and
36 deaf, in addition to any other expenditures for such person, for the
37 purpose of providing community inclusion services through specialized
38 public and private entities from which such person can benefit. The
39 commissioner may determine the criteria by which a person is eligible
40 to receive specialized services and may adopt regulations necessary to
41 carry out the provisions of this subsection. For purposes of this
42 subsection, "community inclusion services" means the assistance
43 provided to persons with disabilities to enable them to connect with
44 their peers without disabilities and with the community at large.
