General Assembly Substitute Bill No. 5329
February Session, 2026
AN ACT ESTABLISHING THE CONNECTICUT EDUCATION
INNOVATION GRANT PROGRAM.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General
Assembly convened:
1 Section 1. (NEW) (Effective July 1, 2026) (a) For the fiscal year ending
2 June 30, 2027, and each fiscal year thereafter, the Department of
3 Education shall establish a competitive grant program to be known as
4 the "Connecticut Education Innovation Grant Program" to fund
5 educational innovations in schools. When awarding grants under said
6 grant program, the department shall prioritize education innovation
7 project proposals that are designed to improve one or more of the
8 following: (1) Early literacy and numeracy outcomes for students from
9 birth to grade eight, inclusive, (2) access to and the impact of high-
10 dosage tutoring in reading and mathematics, (3) college, career and
11 service navigation for students and families, (4) career and early college
12 pathways for students in middle school and high school, (5) effective
13 family and community engagement, including, but not limited to,
14 language access, (6) staffing and talent management models, including,
15 but not limited to, differentiated compensation, master educator and
16 team-teaching models, (7) district and administrative structures, and (8)
17 play-based learning, as defined in section 10-24f of the general statutes.
18 (b) Any local or regional board of education, regional educational
19 service center, governing council of a charter school or other public or

37 (d) (1) Any applicant for a grant under the Connecticut Education
38 Innovation Grant Program may request a waiver from the
39 Commissioner of Education of one or more statutory or regulatory
40 requirements that such recipient demonstrates are a significant barrier
41 to the design or implementation of the education innovation project.
42 (2) The commissioner may grant such waiver request for a specified
43 period of time, not to exceed the duration of the grant, if the
44 commissioner finds the (A) waiver is necessary to carry out the project,
45 (B) project is designed to improve student outcomes, and (C) applicant
46 established clear and measurable goals for the project. The
47 commissioner shall not waive the provisions of part I of chapter 166 of
48 the general statutes, chapters 169 and 172 of the general statutes and
49 sections 10-14n to 10-14w, inclusive, 10-15, 10-16, 10-16b, 10-76d, 10-186,
50 10-221a, 10-223e, 10-226a to 10-226h, inclusive, and 10-233c of the
51 general statutes, any requirement related to a state-approved literacy or
52 numeracy instructional model or any requirement of federal law.

53 (3) The commissioner may terminate a waiver prior to the expiration
54 of the waiver period or grant period if the commissioner determines that
55 (A) an applicant has failed to make satisfactory progress toward the
56 goals of the project, or (B) continuation of the project is inconsistent with
57 state education goals.
58 (e) (1) Each recipient of a grant under the Connecticut Education
59 Innovation Grant Program shall submit, at such time and in such
60 manner as specified by the Department of Education, a report to the
61 department on the implementation and results of the education
62 innovation project, including, but not limited to, data on student
63 outcomes disaggregated by grade, school and student subgroup in a
64 manner that complies with the requirements of the Family Educational
65 Rights and Privacy Act, 20 USC 1232g, as amended from time to time.
66 Such report shall be submitted not less than annually, until at least one
67 year after the termination of the grant period.
68 (2) Not later than January 1, 2028, and annually thereafter, the
69 Department of Education shall submit, in accordance with the
70 provisions of section 11-4a of the general statutes, to the joint standing
71 committee of the General Assembly having cognizance of matters
72 relating to education a report summarizing the grants awarded,
73 outcomes achieved and promising practices identified under the
74 Connecticut Education Innovation Grant Program during the prior
75 fiscal year.
76 (f) There is established an account to be known as the "Connecticut
77 education innovation fund", which shall be a separate, nonlapsing
78 account. The account shall contain any moneys required by law to be
79 deposited in the account. Moneys in the account shall be expended by
80 the Department of Education for the purposes of the Connecticut
81 Education Innovation Grant Program.
