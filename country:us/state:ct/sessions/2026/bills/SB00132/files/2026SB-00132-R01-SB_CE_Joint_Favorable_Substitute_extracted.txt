General Assembly Substitute Bill No. 132
February Session, 2026
AN ACT ESTABLISHING THE CONNECTICUT-GERMANY TRADE
COMMISSION.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General
Assembly convened:
1 Section 1. (NEW) (Effective from passage) (a) There is established a
2 Connecticut-Germany Trade Commission, which shall be part of the
3 Legislative Department.
4 (b) The commission shall consist of the following members:
5 (1) Two appointed by the speaker of the House of Representatives;
6 (2) Two appointed by the president pro tempore of the Senate;
7 (3) Two appointed by the majority leader of the House of
8 Representatives;
9 (4) Two appointed by the majority leader of the Senate;
10 (5) Two appointed by the minority leader of the House of
11 Representatives;
12 (6) Two appointed by the minority leader of the Senate;
13 (7) Two members of the joint standing committee of the General
14 Assembly having cognizance of matters relating to commerce, one of

32 (c) The members of the commission shall each have current or past
33 involvement in organizations that promote German affairs or are
34 interested in the well-being of trade relations between the state and
35 Germany.
36 (d) Any member of the commission appointed under subdivisions (1)
37 to (6), inclusive, and subdivision (13) of subsection (b) of this section
38 may be a member of the General Assembly.
39 (e) (1) All initial appointments to the commission shall be made not
40 later than October 1, 2026. Initial appointments made by the Governor,
41 the majority leader of the House of Representatives and the minority
42 leader of the House of Representatives shall terminate on September 30,
43 2029. Initial appointments made by the speaker of the House of

44 Representatives, the president pro tempore of the Senate, the majority

46 on September 30, 2030. The initial and subsequent terms of members
47 appointed by the chairpersons of the joint standing committee of the
48 General Assembly having cognizance of matters relating to commerce
49 shall be coterminous with the terms of the appointing authority for each
50 such member in accordance with the provisions of section 4-1a of the

52 (2) Appointed members of the commission shall serve for four-year
53 terms which shall commence on the date of appointment, except as
54 provided in subdivision (1) of this subsection. Members shall continue
55 to serve until their successors are appointed.
56 (f) The speaker of the House of Representatives and the president pro
57 tempore of the Senate shall select the chairpersons of the commission
58 from among the members of the commission. Such chairpersons shall
59 schedule the first meeting of the commission, which shall be held not
60 later than November 1, 2026.
61 (g) The administrative staff of the joint standing committee of the
62 General Assembly having cognizance of matters relating to consumer
63 protection shall serve as administrative staff of the commission.
64 (h) Any vacancy shall be filled by the appointing authority. Any
65 vacancy occurring other than by expiration of term shall be filled for the
66 balance of the unexpired term.
67 (i) A majority of the commission shall constitute a quorum for the
68 transaction of any business.
69 (j) The members of the commission shall serve without
70 compensation, but shall, within the limits of available funds, be
71 reimbursed for expenses necessarily incurred in the performance of
72 their duties.
73 (k) The commission shall have the following powers and duties: (1)

74 To advance bilateral trade and investment between the state and
75 Germany; (2) to initiate joint action on policy issues of mutual interest
76 to the state and Germany; (3) to promote business and academic
77 exchanges between the state and Germany; (4) to encourage mutual
78 economic support between the state and Germany; (5) to encourage
79 mutual investment in the infrastructure of the state and Germany; (6) to
80 address such other issues as determined by the commission; (7) to obtain
81 from any executive department, board, commission or other agency of
82 the state such assistance and data as are necessary and available to carry
83 out the purposes of this section; (8) to accept any gift, donation or
84 bequest for the purpose of performing the duties described in this
85 section; and (9) to perform such other acts as may be necessary and
86 appropriate to carry out the duties described in this section.
87 (l) The commission shall meet quarterly, or as often as deemed
88 necessary by the chairpersons or a majority of the commission. Any
89 appointed member who fails to attend three consecutive meetings or
90 who fails to attend fifty per cent of all meetings held during any
91 calendar year shall be deemed to have resigned from the commission.
92 (m) Not later than February 1, 2028, and annually thereafter, the
93 commission shall submit a report, in accordance with the provisions of
94 section 11-4a of the general statutes, to the Governor, the Commissioner
95 of Economic and Community Development and the joint standing
96 committee of the General Assembly having cognizance of matters
97 relating to commerce on the activities of the commission during the
98 preceding year, which shall include, but need not be limited to, any
99 recommendations for policy changes and amendments to the general
100 statutes necessary to effectuate the purposes of the commission.
