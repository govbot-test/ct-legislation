29 Sec. 3. (NEW) (Effective July 1, 2026) (a) The Commissioner of Social
30 Services may periodically review available data on the clinical
31 effectiveness of outpatient prescription drugs covered under the
32 Medicaid program that are projected to exceed (1) a net cost per
33 consumer, after factoring in rebates, of twenty-five thousand dollars per
34 year, or (2) an annual aggregate cost, after factoring in rebates to the
35 medical assistance program, of ten million dollars. The commissioner
36 may, within available appropriations, contract with a third party to
37 conduct a comparative effectiveness review of any such outpatient
38 prescription drug. For purposes of this section, "rebate" means an
39 amount sent to the state by a prescription drug manufacturer to offset
40 the cost of outpatient prescription drugs covered by the Medicaid
41 program.
42 (b) Any such comparative effectiveness review shall include, but
43 need not be limited to: (1) Clinical efficacy and outcomes; (2)
44 information relating to the pricing of the outpatient prescription drug,
45 including, but not limited to, information relating to prices paid by other
46 states or developed nations; (3) such drug's net price to the Medicaid
47 program as compared to its therapeutic benefits, including, but not
48 limited to, the seriousness and prevalence of the disease or condition
49 that is treated by the drug; (4) the extent of utilization of such drug; (5)
50 the likelihood that the use of such drug will reduce the need for other
