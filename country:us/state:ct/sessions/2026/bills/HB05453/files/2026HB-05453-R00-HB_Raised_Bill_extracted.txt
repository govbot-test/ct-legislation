AN ACT REQUIRING A STUDY REGARDING STATE-WIDE
ESSENTIAL WORKER AND FIRST RESPONDER JOB
CLASSIFICATIONS.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General
Assembly convened:
1 Section 1. (Effective from passage) The Commissioner of Administrative
2 Services, in consultation with the Commissioner of Emergency Services
3 and Public Protection and the Labor Commissioner, shall conduct a
4 study to (1) determine whether a state-wide "essential worker" or "first
5 responder" job classification should be established, and (2) if so, (A)
6 determine what effect, if any, such classifications would have on the jobs
7 that fit within such classifications, including, but not limited to, any
8 effect on employee benefits or the requirements applicable to such jobs,
9 (B) develop definitions of such classifications, and (C) determine
10 whether telecommunicators, as defined in section 28-30 of the general
11 statutes, and employees of public works departments, as defined in
12 section 31-57s of the general statutes, should be classified as essential
13 workers or first responders. Not later than January 1, 2027, the
14 Commissioner of Administrative Services shall submit a report, in
15 accordance with the provisions of section 11-4a of the general statutes,
