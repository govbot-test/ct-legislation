Explanation
The bill allows the Department of Correction (DOC) to provide
certain breast cancer services for women in DOC custody at licensed
health care institutions closer to the correctional facility than the UConn
Health Center, resulting in a revenue loss to UConn Health and a net
potential savings to DOC beginning in FY 27, to the extent that DOC is
able to provide these services to women more efficiently.
The Out Years
The annualized ongoing fiscal impact identified above would
continue into the future subject to the continued feasibility of providing
equivalent breast cancer services at closer health care institutions.

SUMMARY
This bill authorizes the Department of Correction (DOC) to arrange
for breast cancer screening, diagnostic, and treatment services for
women in DOC custody to occur at licensed health care institutions that
are closer to the correctional facility than is the UConn Health Center.
It also allows DOC, if it is unable to provide on-site mammograms,
to arrange for them at licensed health care institutions that are closer
than UConn Health Center.
EFFECTIVE DATE: July 1, 2026

institution for females).
