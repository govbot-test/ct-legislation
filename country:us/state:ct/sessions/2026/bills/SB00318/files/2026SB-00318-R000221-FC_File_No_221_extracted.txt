OLR Bill Analysis
SB 318
AN ACT CONCERNING ANIMAL WELFARE.
SUMMARY
This bill expands the state veterinarian’s authority to issue orders for
preventing disease spread among animals and poultry by allowing her
to issue them for any disease, rather than just for those that are
contagious and infectious. It correspondingly allows her to protect the
public from this broader category of disease.
The bill also requires the Department of Agriculture (DoAg)
commissioner to (1) study how the need for companion animal
placement affects domestic violence victims and (2) create a working
group to study the state’s animal population control program.
EFFECTIVE DATE: October 1, 2026, except the working group
provision is effective upon passage.
COMPANION ANIMAL PLACEMENT STUDY
The bill requires the DoAg commissioner, in consultation with the
Office of Victim Services, to study how the need for companion animal
placement affects domestic violence victims and submit a report on the
study and any recommendations to the Environment Committee by
January 1, 2027.
Under the bill, the study must:
1. include resources currently available for domestic violence
victims to find temporary or permanent placements;
2. identify at least one contact person for victims in Connecticut
who need to place their companion animal in another person’s
care due to a domestic violence situation; and

ANIMAL POPULATION CONTROL PROGRAM WORKING GROUP
The bill requires the DoAg commissioner to create, by July 1, 2026, a
working group to study the state’s animal population control program,
which funds spaying or neutering for dogs and cats. The group must
make recommendations about the program, including ways to increase
and stabilize its funding, and the commissioner must submit them to the
Environment Committee by January 1, 2027.
The working group’s members include the following:
1. the DoAg commissioner or his designee;
2. the Connecticut Humane Society’s executive director or his
designee;
3. an animal welfare organization representative, appointed by the
commissioner;
4. a Connecticut Veterinary Medical Association representative;
5. an animal control officers’ organization representative; and
6. any other members the commissioner determines are needed.
