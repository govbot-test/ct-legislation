General Assembly Substitute Bill No. 5159
February Session, 2026
AN ACT FUNDING JOURNALISM FELLOWSHIPS FOR GRADUATES
OF PUBLIC INSTITUTIONS OF HIGHER EDUCATION.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General
Assembly convened:
1 Section 1. (NEW) (Effective July 1, 2026) (a) As used in this section,
2 "local news organization" means a news organization, as defined in
3 section 54-33i of the general statutes, that is located in the state.
4 (b) For the fiscal year ending June 30, 2027, and each fiscal year
5 thereafter, the Office of Policy and Management shall enter into a
6 memorandum of understanding with a student journalism collaborative
7 for such collaborative to establish a journalism fellowship program to
8 fund not fewer than twelve journalism fellowships at one or more local
9 news organizations. The memorandum of understanding shall specify
10 the (1) minimum requirements for a fellowship funded under such
11 program, including, but not limited to, opportunities for (A) a
12 mentorship from experienced journalists and editors, (B) direct work
13 experience, and (C) professional networking, (2) eligibility requirements
14 for individuals to qualify for such journalism fellowships, including, but
15 not limited to, (A) graduating from a journalism degree program at a
16 public institution of higher education in the state, and (B) interviewing
17 with and being approved by the local news organization providing such
18 fellowship, (3) amount at which such fellowships shall be funded, and
19 (4) requirement for the collaborative to post, not later than September 1,
