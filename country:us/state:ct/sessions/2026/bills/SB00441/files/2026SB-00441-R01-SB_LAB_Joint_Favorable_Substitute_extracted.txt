General Assembly Substitute Bill No. 441
February Session, 2026
AN ACT ESTABLISHING A TASK FORCE TO STUDY LABOR LAW
VIOLATIONS BY LIMITED LIABILITY COMPANIES AND OTHER
BUSINESS ENTITIES.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General
Assembly convened:
1 Section 1. (Effective from passage) (a) There is established a task force
2 to study the potential for members of a limited liability company or
3 other business entity to circumvent the provisions of chapter 557 or 558
4 of the general statutes by establishing other business entities.
5 (b) The task force shall consist of the following members:
6 (1) Two appointed by the speaker of the House of Representatives;
7 (2) Two appointed by the president pro tempore of the Senate;
8 (3) One appointed by the majority leader of the House of
9 Representatives;
10 (4) One appointed by the majority leader of the Senate;
11 (5) One appointed by the minority leader of the House of
12 Representatives;
13 (6) One appointed by the minority leader of the Senate;

14 (7) The Labor Commissioner, or the commissioner's designee;
15 (8) The Secretary of the State, or the secretary's designee;
16 (9) The Commissioner of Revenue Services, or the commissioner's
17 designee; and
18 (10) The Commissioner of Administrative Services, or the
19 commissioner's designee.
20 (c) Any member of the task force appointed under subdivision (1),
21 (2), (3), (4), (5) or (6) of subsection (b) of this section may be a member
22 of the General Assembly.
23 (d) All initial appointments to the task force shall be made not later
24 than thirty days after the effective date of this section. Any vacancy shall
25 be filled by the appointing authority.
26 (e) The speaker of the House of Representatives and the president pro
27 tempore of the Senate shall select the chairpersons of the task force from
28 among the members of the task force. Such chairpersons shall schedule
29 the first meeting of the task force, which shall be held not later than sixty
30 days after the effective date of this section.
31 (f) The administrative staff of the joint standing committee of the
32 General Assembly having cognizance of matters relating to labor and
33 public employees shall serve as administrative staff of the task force.
34 (g) Not later than January 1, 2027, the task force shall submit a report
35 on its findings and recommendations to the joint standing committee of
36 the General Assembly having cognizance of matters relating to labor
37 and public employees, in accordance with the provisions of section 11-
38 4a of the general statutes. The task force shall terminate on the date that
39 it submits such report or January 1, 2027, whichever is later.
