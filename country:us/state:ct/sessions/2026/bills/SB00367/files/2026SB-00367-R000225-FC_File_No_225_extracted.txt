AN ACT ESTABLISHING A WORKING GROUP TO STUDY ROADSIDE
SAFETY CONCERNS ASSOCIATED WITH WRECKERS AND TOWING
AND RECOVERY PROFESSIONALS.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General
Assembly convened:
1 Section 1. (Effective from passage) (a) The chairpersons of the joint
2 standing committee of the General Assembly having cognizance of
3 matters relating to public safety and security shall convene a working
4 group to study roadside safety concerns associated with wreckers and
5 towing and recovery professionals. Such study shall include, but need
6 not be limited to, an evaluation of what color and type of lights should
7 be allowed on wreckers and methods to enhance the protection of
8 towing and recovery professionals while such professionals secure
9 wrecked or disabled motor vehicles at roadside scenes.
10 (b) The working group shall include, but need not be limited to, the
11 following members:
12 (1) The chairpersons and ranking members of the joint standing

34 report on its findings and recommendations to the joint standing
35 committee of the General Assembly having cognizance of matters
36 relating to public safety and security, in accordance with the provisions
37 of section 11-4a of the general statutes. The working group shall
38 terminate on the date that it submits such report or January 1, 2027,
39 whichever is later.

Explanation
The bill, which has no fiscal impact, creates a working group to study
roadside safety concerns associated with wreckers and towing and
recovery professionals. It is anticipated that the members of the working
group have the expertise necessary to meet the requirements of the bill.
