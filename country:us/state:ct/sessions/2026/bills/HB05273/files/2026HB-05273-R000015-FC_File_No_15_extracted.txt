2 provisions of any special act, municipal charter or home rule ordinance,

4 while on any public dock, wharf or pier in the state. As used in this
5 section, "personal flotation device" has the same meaning as provided

Department of Energy and GF – Minimal Cost Up to 10,000
Environmental Protection
Note: GF=General Fund
Municipal Impact: None
Explanation
The bill will result in a minimal cost in FY 27, of up to $10,000, to the
Department of Energy and Environmental Protection. The cost is
associated with the purchase of additional personal flotation devices, to
support educational programs run by DEEP, which would be required
for all children under 12 years old, while on any public dock, wharf, or
pier in the state. Additionally, DEEP would require new signage at
public docks, wharfs and piers, to inform the public of the required
change.
The Out Years
There would be minimal outyear costs associated with the
replacement of personal flotation devices.

AND PIERS.
SUMMARY
This bill requires all children under 12 years old to wear a personal
flotation device while on any public dock, wharf, or pier in the state. The
bill specifies that this requirement applies regardless of any existing
special act, municipal charter, or home rule ordinance provisions.
