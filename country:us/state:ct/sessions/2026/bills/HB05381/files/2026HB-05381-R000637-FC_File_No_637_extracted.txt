Explanation
The bill requires the Department of Consumer Protection (DCP) to
oversee the funeral service compensation account (FSCA) and make
grants to eligible applicants resulting in a cost to the state. To meet the
requirements of the bill, DCP will have to hire a paralegal specialist for
a salary and other expenses cost of $76,750 in FY 27 and $74,750 in FY
28, along with associated fringe benefit costs of $31,260 in FY 27 and FY
28. The new employee is needed to review applications, make payments
from the account, and answer applicant questions.

percent of the balance of the FSCA for the costs incurred in managing

OFA Bill Analysis
HB 5381
AN ACT ESTABLISHING AN ACCOUNT TO COMPENSATE VICTIMS
OF UNLAWFUL FUNERAL SERVICE PRACTICES.
SUMMARY:
The bill establishes a separate, non-lapsing account, called the
"Funeral Service Compensation Account." The account must contain (1)
any amounts appropriated or made available by the state, (2) any
monies required to be deposited in the account, and (3) gifts, grants,
donations, or bequests. Investment earnings shall become part of the
assets of the account and the State Treasurer is required to administer
the account.
Under the bill the Department of Consumer Protection (DCP) must
use the funds in the account to make grants to eligible persons who were
victims of fraudulent or unlawful practices when purchasing funeral
services. DCP must establish the requirements for obtaining and issuing
a grant and DCP cannot provide a grant that exceeds the balance of
funds in the account.
Lastly, the bill allows DCP to deduct and retain up to two percent of
the balance in the account for costs incurred by the department for
administering the account.
EFFECTIVE DATE: July 1, 2026
