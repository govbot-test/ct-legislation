AN ACT MAKING TECHNICAL CHANGES TO STATUTES
CONCERNING STATE CONTRACTING.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General
Assembly convened:
1 Section 1. Subdivision (5) of section 4-250 of the general statutes is
2 repealed and the following is substituted in lieu thereof (Effective October
3 1, 2026):
4 (5) "Large state contract" means an agreement or a combination or
5 series of agreements between a state agency or a quasi-public agency
6 and a person, firm or corporation, having a total value of more than five
7 hundred thousand dollars in a calendar or fiscal year, for (A) a project
8 for the construction, alteration or repair of any public building or public
9 work, (B) services, including, but not limited to, consulting and
10 professional services, (C) the procurement of supplies, materials or
11 equipment, (D) a lease, or (E) a licensing arrangement. [The term "large
12 state contract"] "Large state contract" does not include a contract
13 between a state agency or a quasi-public agency and a political

18 (b) The commissioner may designate projects to be accomplished on
19 a total cost basis for (1) new facilities to provide for the substantial space

29 panels established in this subdivision. All contracts for such designated
30 projects shall be based on competitive proposals received by the
31 commissioner, who shall give notice of such project, and specifications
32 for the project, by posting notice on the State Contracting Portal. No
33 contract which includes the construction, reconstruction, alteration,
34 remodeling, repair or demolition of any public building for work by the
35 state for which the total cost is estimated to be more than five hundred
36 thousand dollars may be awarded to a person who is not prequalified
37 for the work, in accordance with the provisions of section 4a-100. The
38 commissioner shall determine all other requirements and conditions for
39 such proposals and awards and shall have sole responsibility for all
40 other aspects of such contracts. Such contracts shall state clearly the
41 responsibilities of the developer to deliver a completed and acceptable
42 product on a date certain, the maximum cost of the project and, as a
43 separate item, the cost of site acquisition, if applicable. No such contract
44 may be entered into by the commissioner without the prior approval of
45 the State Properties Review Board and unless funding has been
