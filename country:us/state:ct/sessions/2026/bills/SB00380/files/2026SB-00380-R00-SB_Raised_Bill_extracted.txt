59 Sec. 2. (NEW) (Effective July 1, 2026) (a) As used in this section,
60 "promise program" means a scholarship program that provides a
61 scholarship in conjunction with services and resources, including, but
62 not limited to, mentoring, peer support networks and internships, to
63 scholarship recipients to ensure the postsecondary academic success of
64 such recipients.
65 (b) (1) Not later than September 1, 2026, and annually thereafter, the
66 Office of Postsecondary Success shall provide a grant to each promise
67 program in the state that was established during or prior to the previous
68 fiscal year. Such grant shall be in an amount equal to three thousand
69 dollars per student enrolled in such promise program.
70 (2) Not later than July 1, 2027, and annually thereafter, each promise
71 program that receives a grant pursuant to subdivision (1) of this
72 subsection shall report to the Office of Postsecondary Success the rate of
73 graduation, postsecondary program enrollment and job placement
74 achieved by the students enrolled in such promise program during the
75 fiscal year in which such grant was received.
76 (c) The Office of Postsecondary Success shall establish a program to
77 support the establishment of new promise programs throughout the

93 Sec. 3. (NEW) (Effective July 1, 2026) (a) As used in this section:
94 (1) "Net cost of attendance" means the tuition and required fees
95 incurred each academic year by an individual student at an institution
96 of higher education, after subtracting such student's financial aid;
97 (2) "Financial aid" means the sum of all scholarships, grants and
98 federal, state and institutional aid received each academic year by a
99 qualifying student. "Financial aid" does not include any federal, state or
100 private student loans received by a qualifying student;
101 (3) "Qualifying student" means any person who (A) is a resident of
102 the state, (B) attended from grades nine to twelve, inclusive, and
103 graduated from a public or nonpublic high school in the state, with a
104 cumulative attendance rate of not less than ninety per cent, (C) enrolls,
105 not more than one year after graduating from such high school, as a full-
106 time student for the academic year commencing with the fall semester
107 of 2026, or any academic year thereafter, at an institution of higher
108 education in a program leading to a bachelor's degree, (D) is making
