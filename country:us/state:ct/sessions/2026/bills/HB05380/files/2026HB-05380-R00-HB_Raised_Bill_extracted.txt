AN ACT CONCERNING THE OFFICE OF THE CHILD ADVOCATE.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General
Assembly convened:
1 Section 1. Section 46a-13m of the 2026 supplement to the general
2 statutes is repealed and the following is substituted in lieu thereof
3 (Effective October 1, 2026):
4 (a) Notwithstanding any provision of the general statutes concerning
5 the confidentiality of records and information, the Child Advocate may
6 request and shall have access to, including the right to promptly inspect
7 and copy, any records necessary to carry out the responsibilities of the
8 Child Advocate as provided in section 46a-13l. Such records shall be
9 provided to the Child Advocate not later than fourteen days from the
10 date of such request. If the Child Advocate is denied access to any
11 records necessary to carry out said responsibilities, the Child Advocate
12 may issue a subpoena for the production of such records as provided in
13 subsection (c) of this section.
14 (b) In the performance of [his] the Child Advocate's responsibilities
15 under subsection (a) of section 46a-13l the Child Advocate may
16 communicate privately with any child or person who has received, is

28 and give testimony or to produce such evidence, as the case may be.
29 (d) The Child Advocate may apply for and accept grants, gifts and
30 bequests of funds from other states, federal and interstate agencies and
31 independent authorities and private firms, individuals and foundations,
32 for the purpose of carrying out [his] the Child Advocate's
33 responsibilities. There is established a child advocate account, which
34 shall be a separate, nonlapsing account. Any funds received under this
35 subsection shall, upon deposit in the General Fund, be credited to said
36 account and may be used by the Child Advocate in the performance of
37 [his] the Child Advocate's duties.
