AN ACT REQUIRING A STUDY OF THE DESIGN OF RESIDENTIAL
ELECTRIC CUSTOMER BILLS.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General
Assembly convened:
1 Section 1. (Effective October 1, 2026) (a) The chairperson of the Public
2 Utilities Regulatory Authority, in consultation with the Consumer
3 Counsel, shall conduct a study that analyzes the depiction and
4 description of charges on electric bills for residential electric customers.
5 Such study shall consider (1) whether such charges are adequately
6 depicted and described to inform a customer of the components of each
7 such charge, (2) whether moving any component of any such charge into
8 another charge would enhance a customer's understanding of such
9 component or more accurately reflect the nature of such component,
10 and (3) any other methods to increase transparency concerning the costs
11 and benefits of programs funded through such charges.
12 (b) If the chairperson finds that the depiction or description of any
13 such charge does not contain adequate information, or that any

OFA Fiscal Note
State Impact: None
Municipal Impact: None
Explanation
The bill requires the Public Utilities Regulatory Authority (PURA), in
consultation with the Consumer Counsel (OCC), to conduct a study
analyzing the description of charges on electric bills for residential
electric customers. This will not result in a fiscal impact to the state as
both PURA and OCC have the staff and expertise necessary to complete
the study.

State Impact: None
