AN ACT REQUIRING TRAINING FOR HOMEMAKER-COMPANION
AGENCY EMPLOYEES.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General
Assembly convened:
1 Section 1. (NEW) (Effective from passage) (a) On and after December
2 31, 2026, each homemaker-companion agency, as defined in section 20-
3 670 of the general statutes, shall provide not less than ten paid hours of
4 initial training to each new employee not later than ninety days after a
5 new employee's start of employment. Such initial training shall include,
6 but need not be limited to, programs relating to the topics described in
7 subdivisions (1), (3), (4) and (5) of subsection (b) of this section and, if
8 the new employee will provide services to persons with Alzheimer's
9 disease or dementia, subdivision (7) of subsection (b) of this section.
10 (b) Not later than December 31, 2026, the Commissioner of Consumer
11 Protection, in consultation with the Commissioners of Public Health,
12 Social Services, Developmental Services and Mental Health and
13 Addiction Services, shall develop a list of approved homemaker-
14 companion agency employee training programs appropriate for use by
15 homemaker-companion agencies when providing mandatory employee

16 training pursuant to the provisions of subsections (a) and (d) of this
17 section. The list of approved homemaker-companion agency employee
18 training programs shall be limited to programs concerning (1)

28 homemaker-companion agency shall not require a new employee who
29 (A) is currently certified in cardiopulmonary resuscitation, (B) has
30 completed a course in first aid, and (C) submits proof of such
31 certification and completion to complete initial training relating to
32 cardiopulmonary resuscitation and first aid.
33 (d) On and after January 1, 2027, each homemaker-companion agency
34 shall provide annually not less than ten paid hours of continuing
35 education to each employee. Such continuing education shall be
36 comprised of programs on the list of approved homemaker-companion
37 agency employee training programs developed pursuant to subsection
38 (b) of this section. Each homemaker-companion agency shall ensure that
39 each employee completes every program on such list at least once every
40 two calendar years.
41 (e) Upon completion of any employee training conducted pursuant
42 to subsections (a) and (d) of this section, each homemaker-companion
43 agency and employee shall complete a form, in a form and manner
44 prescribed by the Commissioner of Consumer Protection, attesting that
45 the employee completed such training. Such form shall include the
46 names of the homemaker-companion agency and employee, the
47 training programs completed by the employee and the signatures of a

Section 1 from passage New section
Statement of Purpose:
To require homemaker-companion agencies to provide not less than ten
hours of (1) initial training to new employees, and (2) continuing
education to current employees.
