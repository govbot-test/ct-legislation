sSB 458
AN ACT DESIGNATING VARIOUS DAYS AND MONTHS.
SUMMARY
This bill requires the governor to proclaim the following:
1. January 11 to be Hartford Whalers Day, to commemorate the first
Whalers game played in Hartford;
2. February 3 to be Topical Steroid Withdrawal Awareness Day, to
raise awareness of this adverse reaction characterized by intense
burning, red inflamed skin, and peeling that happens after
stopping long-term or high-potency topical steroid creams;
3. March 4 to be 6888th Central Postal Directory Battalion Day, to
recognize the only African-American all-female unit sent
overseas during World War II to process millions of pieces of
backlogged mail for troops in the European theater;
4. March 8 to be Henry Breault Day, to remember the late Medal of
Honor recipient from Putnam who was the first submariner to be
presented the award on March 8, 1924, for his actions aboard a
U.S. submarine that was sinking, when he returned to the
torpedo room to rescue a shipmate and was trapped in the
submarine for 31 hours;
5. the 1st day of the month of Shawwal, the 10th month in the
Islamic lunar calendar, as Eid al-Fitr to mark the end of Ramadan;
6. the month of May to be Progressive Supranuclear Palsy
Awareness Month, to raise awareness about the presentation of
and available treatments for this rare progressive
neurodegenerative disorder affecting movement, balance, vision,

and speech due to protein buildup in the brain;
7. July 17 to be Hiram Bingham IV Day, to remember the late

10. September 30 to be Military Suicide Remembrance and
Awareness Day, to honor veterans and military members lost to
suicide and raise awareness of mental health issues among
veterans and military members and the available resources and
supports for them.
The bill also (1) renames Indian Day as Indigenous People’s Day,
celebrated on the last Friday in September to commemorate Native
Americans and their contribution to American life and civilization and
(2) moves Lobster Roll Day to the third Sunday, instead of the third
Saturday, of September.
Under the bill, suitable observance exercises may be held in the State
Capitol and elsewhere as the governor designates.
