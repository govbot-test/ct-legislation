OLR Bill Analysis
SB 370
AN ACT CONCERNING THE HOURLY COMPENSATION FOR
ADJUNCT FIRE INSTRUCTORS.
SUMMARY
This bill requires the Department of Administrative Services
commissioner, beginning July 1, 2026, and every fiscal year after, to
adjust a Connecticut Fire Academy adjunct fire instructor’s hourly
compensation by the same percentage as the general wage increase and
cost-of-living adjustment included in the most recently ratified
agreement between the state and the State Employees Bargaining Agent
Coalition.
EFFECTIVE DATE: July 1, 2026
BACKGROUND
Adjunct Fire Instructors
Generally, adjunct fire instructors are per diem (paid daily)
instructors at the Connecticut Fire Academy and must complete at least
80 hours of work a year. Their duties include (1) preparing for and
conducting fire service training from a prepared curriculum, (2)
adapting the course content and curricula and preparing lesson plans,
and (3) maintaining tools and equipment utilized in training.
