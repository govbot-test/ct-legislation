AN ACT ESTABLISHING A BRIDGE PROGRAM FOR EMERGENCY
TREATMENT AND RECOVERY NAVIGATION FOR PERSONS WITH
AN OPIOID USE DISORDER.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General
Assembly convened:
1 Section 1. (NEW) (Effective October 1, 2026) (a) As used in this section:
2 (1) "Buprenorphine" means a synthetic opiate with partial agonist
3 actions used in the treatment of opioid use disorder;
4 (2) "Opioid antagonist" means naloxone hydrochloride or any other
5 similarly acting and equally safe drug approved by the federal Food and
6 Drug Administration for the treatment of a drug overdose;
7 (3) "Opioid use disorder" means a medical condition characterized by
8 a problematic pattern of opioid use and misuse leading to clinically
9 significant impairment or distress; and
10 (4) "Treatment program" means a program operated by the
11 Department of Mental Health and Addiction Services or approved by
12 the Commissioner of Mental Health and Addiction Services for

14 or for the detoxification of a drug-dependent person, as defined in

16 (b) On and after January 1, 2027, each hospital licensed pursuant to
17 chapter 368v of the general statutes shall (1) administer buprenorphine

This act shall take effect as follows and shall amend the following
sections:
Section 1 October 1, 2026 New section
Statement of Purpose:
To require hospitals to (1) treat patients presenting to the emergency
department with symptoms of opioid use disorder with buprenorphine
without requiring admission of the patient to the hospital, (2) discharge
such patients from the emergency department with a supply of opioid
antagonists, and (3) refer such patients to local substance use disorder
treatment programs.
