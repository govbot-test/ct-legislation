AN ACT ESTABLISHING THE CONNECTICUT EDUCATION
INNOVATION GRANT PROGRAM.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General
Assembly convened:
1 Section 1. (NEW) (Effective July 1, 2026) (a) For the fiscal year ending
2 June 30, 2027, and each fiscal year thereafter, the Department of
3 Education shall establish a competitive grant program to be known as
4 the "Connecticut Education Innovation Grant Program" to fund
5 educational innovations in schools. When awarding grants under said
6 grant program, the department shall prioritize education innovation
7 project proposals that are designed to improve one or more of the
8 following: (1) Early literacy and numeracy outcomes for students from
9 birth to grade eight, inclusive, (2) access to and the impact of high-
10 dosage tutoring in reading and mathematics, (3) college, career and
11 service navigation for students and families, (4) career and early college
12 pathways for students in middle school and high school, (5) effective
13 family and community engagement, including, but not limited to,
14 language access, and (6) any priority areas established by the
15 Commissioner of Education that align with the goals of improving
16 educational access and outcomes.

17 (b) Any local or regional board of education, regional educational
18 service center, governing council of a charter school or other public or
19 nonprofit education provider approved by the Commissioner of
20 Education may apply, in the form and manner specified by the
21 commissioner, for a grant under the Connecticut Education Innovation
22 Grant Program to plan, implement or scale an education innovation
23 project. Each grant application shall include, but need not be limited to,
24 (1) a description of the education challenge to be addressed and the
25 student population to be served, (2) a plan of action and description of
26 the proposed activities, (3) the baseline data and proposed outcome
27 measures, including, but not limited to, targets for student achievement
28 and other relevant indicators, (4) implementation steps and timelines,
29 and (5) a plan for evaluating the effectiveness of and sharing lessons
30 from the project.
31 (c) The Department of Education shall award grants under the
32 Connecticut Education Innovation Grant Program for the following
33 durations: (1) A period of not more than one year for planning grants,
34 and (2) a period of not more than three years for implementation and
35 scale grants.
36 (d) (1) Any applicant for a grant under the Connecticut Education
37 Innovation Grant Program may request a waiver from the
38 Commissioner of Education of one or more statutory or regulatory
39 requirements that such recipient demonstrates are a significant barrier
40 to the design or implementation of the education innovation project.
41 (2) The commissioner may grant such waiver request for a specified
42 period of time, not to exceed the duration of the grant, if the
43 commissioner finds the (A) waiver is necessary to carry out the project,
44 (B) project is designed to improve student outcomes, and (C) applicant
45 established clear and measurable goals for the project. The
46 commissioner shall not waive any requirement related to student health
47 and safety, civil rights, special education, student data privacy or
48 educator certification standards.

49 (3) The commissioner may terminate a waiver prior to the expiration
50 of the waiver period or grant period if the commissioner determines that
51 (A) an applicant has failed to make satisfactory progress toward the
52 goals of the project, or (B) continuation of the project is inconsistent with
53 state education goals.
54 (e) (1) Each recipient of a grant under the Connecticut Education
55 Innovation Grant Program shall submit, at such time and in such
56 manner as specified by the Department of Education, a report to the
57 department on the implementation and results of the education
58 innovation project, including, but not limited to, data on student
59 outcomes disaggregated by grade, school and student subgroup in a
60 manner that complies with the requirements of the Family Educational
61 Rights and Privacy Act, 20 USC 1232g, as amended from time to time.
62 Such report shall be submitted not less than annually, until at least one
63 year after the termination of the grant period.
64 (2) Not later than January 1, 2028, and annually thereafter, the
65 Department of Education shall submit, in accordance with the
66 provisions of section 11-4a of the general statutes, to the joint standing
67 committee of the General Assembly having cognizance of matters
68 relating to education a report summarizing the grants awarded,
69 outcomes achieved and promising practices identified under the
70 Connecticut Education Innovation Grant Program during the prior
71 fiscal year.
72 (f) There is established an account to be known as the "Connecticut
73 education innovation fund", which shall be a separate, nonlapsing
74 account. The account shall contain any moneys required by law to be
75 deposited in the account. Moneys in the account shall be expended by
76 the Department of Education for the purposes of the Connecticut
77 Education Innovation Grant Program.
