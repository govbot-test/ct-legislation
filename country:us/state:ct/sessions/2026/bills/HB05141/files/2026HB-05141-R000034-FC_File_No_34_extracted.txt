OLR Bill Analysis
HB 5141
AN ACT REQUIRING FEAR OF RETALIATION TRAINING FOR
PERSONS PROVIDING ASSISTED LIVING SERVICES IN MANAGED
RESIDENTIAL COMMUNITIES.
SUMMARY
This bill requires each licensed assisted living services agency (ALSA)
that provides services to a managed residential community (MRC) to
make sure the agency’s employees receive annual in-service training
about residents’ fears that employees, and others, will retaliate against
them for making complaints about their care.
The training must at least include (1) a discussion about residents’
rights to file complaints and voice grievances; (2) examples of what
might be, or be perceived as, an employee’s retaliation against a
resident; and (3) ways to prevent employee retaliation and ease
residents’ fears about it.
Under the bill, the person leading the training must be familiar with
the needs of the resident population the agency serves at the MRC. But
this person does not need to be a qualified social worker or social worker
consultant.
EFFECTIVE DATE: October 1, 2026
BACKGROUND
ALSAs
Under existing law, the state does not license assisted living facilities.
Instead, it licenses and regulates ALSAs that provide assisted living
services. These services include nursing services and assistance with
activities of daily living. ALSAs can only provide these services at an
MRC or certain federally-funded elderly housing complexes.
