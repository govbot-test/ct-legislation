AN ACT REQUIRING CERTAIN HEALTH CARE FACILITIES TO
ALLOW TERMINALLY ILL QUALIFYING PATIENTS TO USE
CANNABIS.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General
Assembly convened:
1 Section 1. (NEW) (Effective October 1, 2026) (a) As used in this section:
2 (1) "Cannabis" means marijuana, as defined in section 21a-240 of the
3 general statutes;
4 (2) "Health care facility" means a (A) hospital or nursing home, as
5 such terms are defined in section 19a-490 of the general statutes, or (B)
6 hospice inpatient facility or short-term hospital special hospice, licensed
7 pursuant to chapter 368v of the general statutes;
8 (3) "Qualifying patient" means a "qualifying patient", as defined in
9 section 21a-408 of the general statutes, who (A) has obtained a valid
10 certificate of registration pursuant to subsection (a) of section 21a-408d
11 of the general statutes, and (B) is terminally ill; and
12 (4) "Terminally ill" means a medical condition resulting in a

16 (b) Except as provided in subsection (c) of this section, health care
17 facility shall allow a qualifying patient to use cannabis within the health
18 care facility and shall:
19 (1) Prohibit smoking or vaping as methods to use cannabis;
20 (2) Include the use of cannabis within a qualifying patient's medical

22 (3) Require a qualifying patient to provide a copy of a qualifying
23 patient's certificate of registration, issued pursuant to subsection (a) of
24 section 21a-408d of the general statutes;
25 (4) Reasonably restrict the manner in which a qualifying patient
26 stores and uses cannabis, including, but not limited to, requiring the
27 cannabis to be stored in a locked container, to ensure the safety of other
28 patients, guests and employees of the health care facility, in compliance
29 with other provisions of the general statutes and the safe operations of
30 the health care facility; and
31 (5) Develop and disseminate written guidelines for the use of
32 cannabis by qualifying patients within the health care facility.
33 (c) Notwithstanding the provisions of this section, a qualifying
34 patient receiving emergency care shall not be permitted to use cannabis
35 while the qualifying patient is receiving emergency care.
36 (d) (1) If a federal regulatory agency, the United States Department
37 of Justice or the federal Centers for Medicare and Medicaid Services
38 takes one of the following actions, a health care facility may suspend
39 compliance with the provisions of this section until the regulatory
40 agency, the United States Department of Justice or the federal Centers
41 for Medicare and Medicaid Services notifies the health care facility that
