one, modifying the required content, and adding the Labor and Public
Employees Committee as a recipient. This does not result in any fiscal
impact to the state or municipalities.

OLR Bill Analysis
SB 349
AN ACT CONCERNING MODIFICATIONS TO THE FIREFIGHTERS
CANCER RELIEF FUND.
SUMMARY
This bill makes changes to the state treasurer’s reporting
requirements on programs that provide benefits to firefighters with
cancer.
Current law requires the treasurer to issue separate annual reports on
the status of the Firefighters Cancer Relief Program (due each January)
and a separate program that provides workers’ compensation-like
benefits to firefighters with cancer (due each July). The bill combines
these two reporting requirements into one annual report, with the first
combined report due by October 1, 2026. It also changes the report’s
required contents to include the projected and actual (1) expenditures
(rather than participation) for each program and (2) income sources for
the firefighters cancer relief account (rather than certain demographic
information about the firefighters receiving benefits).
The bill also (1) requires the treasurer to submit the report to the
Labor and Public Employees Committee (in addition to the Firefighters
Cancer Relief Fund Advisory Committee, which receives it under
current law) and (2) makes technical and conforming changes.
EFFECTIVE DATE: October 1, 2026
