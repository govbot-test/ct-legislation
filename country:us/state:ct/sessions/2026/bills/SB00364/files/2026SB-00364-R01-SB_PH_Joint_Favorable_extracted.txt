AN ACT CONCERNING EXTREME WEATHER PROTOCOLS.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General
Assembly convened:
1 Section 1. (NEW) (Effective October 1, 2026) Not later than January 1,
2 2027, the Division of Emergency Management and Homeland Security
3 within the Department of Emergency Services and Public Protection
4 shall (1) develop, in consultation with the Office of the Governor and
5 municipal leaders, (A) standardized extreme hot and cold weather
6 protocols that include, but are not limited to, weather factors, such as
7 temperatures, heat index and wind chill, that will prompt the state and
8 municipalities to require the opening of cooling centers and warming
9 centers throughout the state, and (B) methods for accessing
10 transportation to such centers and communicating such protocols to the
11 public, (2) post such protocols on the division's Internet web site, and
12 (3) provide such protocols to the 2-1-1 Infoline program operated by the
13 United Way of Connecticut.
This act shall take effect as follows and shall amend the following
