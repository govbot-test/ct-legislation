AN ACT CONCERNING RESOURCE RECOVERY FACILITIES IN THE
STATE.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General
Assembly convened:
1 Section 1. (Effective from passage) The Commissioner of Energy and
2 Environmental Protection shall convene a working group within the
3 Department of Energy and Environmental Protection to examine the
4 need for and viability and impact of suspending, for a period not to
5 exceed five consecutive fiscal years, the fee described in section 22a-232
6 of the general statutes for one or more resource recovery facilities in the
7 state. Such examination may consider other means to meet any such
8 identified need, including, but not limited to, the availability of state
9 grant, tax abatement or financing programs. Such working group shall,
10 at a minimum, consist of one member from each of the member towns
11 of the Bristol Resource Recovery Facility Operating Committee, the
12 Commissioner of Energy and Environmental Protection, or the
13 commissioner's designee, the Secretary of the Office of Policy and
14 Management, or the secretary's designee, the Commissioner of

OFA Fiscal Note
State Impact: None
Municipal Impact: None
Explanation
The bill, which requires the Department of Energy and
Environmental Protection (DEEP) to convene a working group to study
the need for and viability of suspending one or more resource recovery
facilities in the state, will not result in a fiscal impact as the working
group has the resources and expertise necessary to complete the study.
