214 Sec. 8. (NEW) (Effective from passage) (a) There is established a
215 working group to examine and make recommendations concerning (1)
216 the methods of determining the number of justice of the peace positions
217 in each municipality and statewide; (2) the portability of a justice of the
218 peace appointment in one municipality to other municipalities; (3) the
219 process by which justices of the peace are selected; (4) potential training,
220 minimum qualifications, application processes and background check
221 requirements for justices of the peace; (5) oversight of justices of the
222 peace, including the potential for levying fines; (6) potential legal
223 consequences for misrepresenting a person's status as a justice of the
224 peace; and (7) issues related to human trafficking, forced marriage and
225 marriage fraud.
226 (b) The working group shall consist of the following members:
227 (1) Two appointed by the speaker of the House of Representatives,
228 one of whom is a justice of the peace and one of whom is a representative
229 of a professional organization for justices of the peace;
230 (2) Two appointed by the president pro tempore of the Senate, who
231 are town clerks;
232 (3) One appointed by the majority leader of the House of
233 Representatives, who is a town party chairperson;
234 (4) One appointed by the majority leader of the Senate, who is a
235 justice of the peace and not a member of a major political party;
236 (5) Two appointed by the minority leader of the House of
