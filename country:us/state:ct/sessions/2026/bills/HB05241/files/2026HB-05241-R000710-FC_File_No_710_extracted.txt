SUMMARY
This bill requires the Department of Public Health (DPH)
commissioner, in collaboration with the Department of Energy and
Environmental Protection (DEEP) commissioner, to establish, by
January 1, 2027, a Triple Bottom Line Justice (TBLJ) demonstration pilot
program.
Specifically, the DPH commissioner must (1) create the TBLJ pilot
program in a city with a population over 148,000 according to the most
recent decennial U.S. census (Bridgeport) and (2) contract with an
existing community-led redevelopment organization in the city to run
the program. Under the bill, “TBLJ” is the integration of evidence-based
practice, rule of law, and meaningful community engagement to tackle
health, environmental, and economic challenges faced by children,
families, and communities.
The program must serve as a proof-of-concept for TBLJ programming
related to Brownfields to Healthfields (B2H) that advances healthy, safe,
and vibrant communities. Under the bill, “B2H” is community-led
brownfield redevelopment to transform contaminated properties in
areas with pollution, disease, poverty, and violence into uses that
strengthen children, family and community physical and mental health,
resiliency, equity, and vitality.
The bill requires the TBLJ pilot program to have the following two
goals:
1. describe B2H environmental remediation methods and outcomes

Legislative History
The House referred the bill (File 41) to the Appropriations

implement an education program on and plan to increase the use of
certain diagnostic codes in insurance claims.
