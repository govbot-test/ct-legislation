The bill, which does not have a fiscal impact, requires the Department
of Motor Vehicles to suspend the driver's license of any person who
commits a third or subsequent violation for failing to stop for a school
bus. It is expected that the agency can incorporate this change into
current operations.

OLR Bill Analysis
sSB 508
AN ACT CONCERNING THE SUSPENSION OF AN OPERATOR'S
LICENSE FOR REPEATEDLY FAILING TO STOP FOR A SCHOOL
BUS.
SUMMARY
This bill requires the motor vehicles commissioner, without a
hearing, to issue a six-month suspension of the driver’s license or
operating privilege of someone who, for the third or subsequent time,
violates the law on failing to stop for a school bus. Specifically, this
applies if the person is convicted, forfeited a bond taken, or received a
suspended judgment or sentence for the violation.
By law, drivers generally must (1) stop their vehicles at least 10 feet
from the front or rear of a school bus that is displaying its flashing red
signal lights and (2) remain there until the bus no longer displays the
lights. Any person who violates this law faces (1) a $450 fine for the first
offense and (2) a fine of $500 to $1,000, up to 30 days in prison, or both
for a subsequent offense (CGS § 14-279).
A separate law authorizes municipalities to adopt ordinances for
using camera systems to enforce this law and set fines for violations,
separate from the penalties in state law (CGS § 14-279c). The bill does
not apply to violations of these municipal ordinances.
