Explanation
The bill transfers $100,000 from the General Fund to the neglected
cemetery account within the Office of Policy and Management (OPM).
Municipalities may apply to OPM for grants from this fund to maintain
the graves of wartime veterans.1 This results in a potential revenue gain
for municipalities beginning in FY 27 to the extent that they receive
grants to maintain veterans’ graves. It is anticipated that OPM can
administer this account within available resources.
The Out Years
The annualized ongoing fiscal impact identified above would
continue into the future subject to the number of towns that apply for
grants to maintain veterans’ graves.
1 This is an existing program that is funded from death certificate fees collected by the
Department of Public Health. In 2025, approximately $296,000 was awarded to
municipalities.

sSB 282
AN ACT APPROPRIATING FUNDS TO THE NEGLECTED
CEMETERY ACCOUNT AND CONCERNING CARE AND
MAINTENANCE OF WARTIME VETERANS' GRAVES.
SUMMARY
This bill transfers $100,000 for FY 27 from the General Fund to the
neglected cemetery account and allows account funds to be used as
grants to municipalities to care for and maintain graves for anyone who
served in a time of war.
By law, the Office of Policy and Management may, from the neglected
cemetery account, make grants to municipalities for maintaining
neglected burial grounds and cemeteries with more than six places of
interment (CGS § 19a-308b). The municipal selectmen may use the funds
to clear vegetation, mow lawn areas, repair fences or walls, and fix
memorial stones.
Under existing law, municipalities are responsible for the cost of
caring for and maintaining graves of those who served in wartime if the
services are not paid for by private individuals or the cemetery trustees.
The bill allows municipalities to apply for money available in the
neglected cemetery account for grants for these maintenance purposes.
The bill also makes various minor, technical, and conforming
changes.
