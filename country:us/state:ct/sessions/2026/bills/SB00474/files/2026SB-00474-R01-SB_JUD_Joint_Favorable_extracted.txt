AN ACT CONCERNING THE PROVISION OF ANNUAL TRAINING TO A
PERSON APPOINTED AS A CONSERVATOR IN A PROBATE COURT
PROCEEDING.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General
Assembly convened:
1 Section 1. (NEW) (Effective July 1, 2026) (a) Any person who, on or
2 after October 1, 2026, is appointed as a conservator of the estate or a
3 conservator of the person in a Probate Court proceeding conducted
4 pursuant to chapter 802h of the general statutes shall complete an
5 annual training program established by the Probate Court
6 Administrator pursuant to subsection (b) of this section.
7 (b) The Probate Court Administrator shall, within available
8 appropriations, establish, supervise and fund a program of training for
9 newly appointed conservators of the estate and conservators of the
10 person. The training program shall include, but not be limited to, a
11 review of: (1) Statutory provisions, the Probate Court rules of procedure
12 and regulations adopted by the Probate Court Administrator that are
13 applicable to the responsibilities and duties of a conservator; (2) ethical
14 considerations arising when carrying out the responsibilities and duties

15 of a conservator; (3) the operation of the Probate Court system; (4) the
16 Probate Court User Guide for Conservators; and (5) the resources that
17 are available to a conservator seeking assistance, guidance or advice in
18 carrying out the duties and responsibilities owed to the conserved
19 person.
20 (c) Any person appointed as a conservator of the estate or a
21 conservator of the person in a Probate Court proceeding, on or after
22 October 1, 2026, who without good cause shown, fails to complete the
23 annual training requirements prescribed in this section shall be subject
24 to removal as a conservator in accordance with the provisions of section
25 45a-660 of the general statutes.
26 (d) Any person appointed as a conservator of the estate or a
27 conservator of the person in a Probate Court proceeding, prior to
28 October 1, 2026, may participate in the annual training provided
29 pursuant to this section. The Probate Court Administrator may charge
30 a reasonable fee not to exceed thirty-five dollars for the provision of such
31 training to persons appointed as a conservator of the estate or a
32 conservator of the person prior to October 1, 2026.
