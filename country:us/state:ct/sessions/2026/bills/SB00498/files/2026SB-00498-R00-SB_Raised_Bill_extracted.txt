47 Sec. 2. (Effective from passage) (a) The Secretary of the Office of Policy
48 and Management, in consultation with the Commissioner of Social
49 Services, shall conduct a cost-benefit analysis on (1) transferring fiscal
50 intermediary duties for self-directed home care programs from a private
51 contractor to the state, and (2) authorizing access by personal care
52 attendants not eligible for medical assistance established pursuant to
53 Title XIX or Title XXI of the Social Security Act or the Covered
54 Connecticut program established pursuant to section 19a-754c of the
55 general statutes to a health insurance program subsidized by the state.
56 (b) The cost-benefit analysis shall document the direct and indirect
57 costs, savings, and qualitative and quantitative benefits of state-
58 administered management of duties performed by a privately
59 contracted fiscal intermediary, including, but not limited to:
60 (1) The costs of timesheet processing errors by a privately contracted
61 fiscal intermediary to consumers, personal care attendants and the state
62 and recommendations and benefits relating to ensuring accuracy of
63 timesheet processing;
64 (2) The effects of timesheet processing errors and health insurance
65 costs on the retention rate of personal care attendants and related costs
66 to the state;
67 (3) Effectiveness of communication of eligibility changes for
68 consumers and programmatic changes to personal care attendants and
69 benefits of improving, if warranted, such communication; and
70 (4) Cost benefits to the state of managing fiscal intermediary duties
71 and retaining related infrastructure rather than contracting out such
72 services.

To require contractual safeguards for personal care attendants working

state agencies and private organizations contracting with state agencies.
