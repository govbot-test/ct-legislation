AN ACT CONCERNING A BRIDGE PROGRAM TO ALLOW ACCESS
TO FOOD, HOUSING AND HEALTH CARE BENEFITS.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General
Assembly convened:
1 Section 1. (Effective from passage) (a) As used in this section,
2 "vulnerable persons" means veterans, homeless persons or persons at
3 risk of homelessness, adults diagnosed with autism spectrum disorder,
4 young adults aging out of the foster care system and persons age fifty-
5 five to sixty-four who are at risk of losing benefits under the Medicaid
6 and supplemental nutrition assistance programs and permanent
7 supportive housing assistance due to changes in work requirements
8 pursuant to P.L. 119-21 and proposed changes to United States
9 Department of Housing and Urban Development regulations.
10 (b) Not later than October 1, 2026, the Commissioner of Social
11 Services, in collaboration with the Commissioners of Aging and
12 Disability Services, Children and Families, Housing and Mental Health
13 and Addiction Services and the Labor Commissioner, shall develop a
14 plan for a bridge program to provide state-funded medical assistance,
15 nutrition assistance, supportive housing and employment assistance
