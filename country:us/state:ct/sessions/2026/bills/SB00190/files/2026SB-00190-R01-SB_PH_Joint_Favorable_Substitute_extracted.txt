General Assembly Substitute Bill No. 190
February Session, 2026
AN ACT AUTHORIZING CERTAIN FOREIGN-EDUCATED
VETERINARIANS TO PRACTICE UNDER A TEMPORARY PERMIT
AND THE ESTABLISHMENT OF A VETERINARIAN-CLIENT-PATIENT
RELATIONSHIP THROUGH THE USE OF TELEHEALTH FOR THE
TREATMENT OF URGENT MEDICAL CONDITIONS.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General
Assembly convened:

63 Sec. 2. Subsection (c) of section 20-197 of the general statutes is
64 repealed and the following is substituted in lieu thereof (Effective October
65 1, 2026):
66 (c) [A] (1) Except as provided in subdivision (2) of this subsection, a
67 veterinarian-client-patient relationship may not be established solely
68 through veterinary telemedicine. After a veterinarian-client-patient
69 relationship has been established, such relationship may be maintained
70 through veterinary telemedicine in between medically necessary
71 examinations of the animal by the veterinarian or timely medical visits
72 by the veterinarian to the location where the animal is kept. If there is
73 no veterinarian-client-patient relationship, a veterinarian may only
74 provide general advice using electronic means and shall not provide any
75 specific advice regarding the animal, including, but not limited to, any
76 diagnosis or recommended medical care or treatment of the animal. As
77 used in this subsection, "veterinary telemedicine" means the exchange
78 of medical information regarding the status of an animal's health or the
79 delivery of a medical diagnosis, care or treatment of an animal through
80 electronic means, including, but not limited to, telephone, video, mobile
81 applications or an Internet web site-based platform.
82 (2) A veterinarian-client-patient relationship may be established
83 through veterinary telemedicine only when (A) a veterinarian
