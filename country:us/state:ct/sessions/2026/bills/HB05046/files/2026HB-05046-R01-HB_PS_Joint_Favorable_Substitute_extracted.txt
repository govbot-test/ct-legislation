215 Sec. 4. (NEW) (Effective October 1, 2026) (a) As used in this section,
216 "first responder" means any (1) police officer, as defined in section 7-
217 294a of the general statutes, (2) uniformed member of a paid or
218 volunteer fire department, or (3) emergency medical responder,
219 emergency medical technician, advanced emergency medical
220 technician, emergency medical services instructor or paramedic, as such
221 terms are defined in section 20-206jj of the general statutes.
222 (b) The Connecticut Housing Finance Authority shall develop and
223 administer a program of mortgage assistance to first responders in the
224 state. Such assistance shall be available to an eligible first responder
225 under guidelines adopted by the authority for the purchase of a home

226 used as such first responder's principal residence in the community
227 served by such first responder. In making mortgage assistance available
228 under the program, the authority may utilize down payment assistance
229 or any other appropriate housing subsidies. The terms of any mortgage
230 assistance may allow the mortgagee to realize a reasonable portion of
231 any equity gain upon sale of the mortgaged property.
232 Sec. 5. (NEW) (Effective January 1, 2027, and applicable to taxable years
233 commencing on or after January 1, 2027) (a) As used in this section, "eligible
234 volunteer firefighter" means an individual who is actively serving as a
235 volunteer firefighter for a fire department in the state and who has
236 served as a volunteer firefighter for a fire department in the state for not
237 less than fifteen consecutive years, as documented by the chief of the
238 department.
239 (b) For taxable years commencing on or after January 1, 2027, each
240 eligible volunteer firefighter shall be allowed a credit against the tax
241 imposed by chapter 229 of the general statutes, other than the liability
242 imposed by section 12-707 of the general statutes. The amount of the
243 credit shall be one thousand dollars. Any taxpayer claiming the credit
244 shall provide to the Department of Revenue Services documentation
245 supporting such claim in the form and manner prescribed by the
246 Commissioner of Revenue Services.
