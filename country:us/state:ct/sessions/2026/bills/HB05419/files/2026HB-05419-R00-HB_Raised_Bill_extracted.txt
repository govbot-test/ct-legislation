AN ACT REQUIRING THE DEPARTMENT OF CHILDREN AND
FAMILIES TO EXPAND ELIGIBILITY FOR POSTSECONDARY
EDUCATION BENEFITS FOR CERTAIN YOUTH.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General
Assembly convened:
1 Section 1. (NEW) (Effective July 1, 2026) (a) As used in this section:
2 (1) "Department" means the Department of Children and Families;
3 (2) "Postsecondary education program" means any program that
4 leads to an academic degree or certification in a vocation or employment
5 training; and
6 (3) "Postsecondary education funding programs" means the program
7 set forth in department policy (A) 28-1 under which the department
8 funds the postsecondary education of a youth who is committed to the
9 department, remains committed at the age of eighteen and meets the
10 requirements established by the department in its policies and
11 regulations, and (B) 25-2 under which the department funds the
12 postsecondary education of a youth who has been adopted on or before

13 such youth's eighteenth birthday and meets the requirements
14 established by the department in its policies and regulations.
15 (b) The Commissioner of Children and Families shall amend the
16 department policies and regulations regarding the postsecondary
17 education funding programs to provide an extension of time for the
18 qualification under such programs of any youth whose postsecondary
19 education was disrupted by the COVID-19 pandemic. Nothing in this
20 section shall be construed to require the department to continue
21 providing case management services for any youth who begins
22 receiving funding under the postsecondary education funding
23 programs when such youth is older than twenty-one years of age.
This act shall take effect as follows and shall amend the following
sections:
Section 1 July 1, 2026 New section
Statement of Purpose:
To require the Department of Children and Families to expand
eligibility of postsecondary education benefits for foster and adopted
youth whose postsecondary education was disrupted by the COVID-19
pandemic.
