General Assembly Substitute Bill No. 428
February Session, 2026
AN ACT CONCERNING RESEARCH FUNDING AT INSTITUTIONS OF
HIGHER EDUCATION IN THE STATE.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General
Assembly convened:
1 Section 1. (NEW) (Effective July 1, 2026) (a) There is established an
2 Academic Research Funding Commission, which shall be part of the
3 Executive Department.
4 (b) The commission shall consist of the following members:
5 (1) One appointed by the speaker of the House of Representatives,
6 who is a member of the faculty at The University of Connecticut;
7 (2) One appointed by the president pro tempore of the Senate, who is
8 a member of the faculty at a state university within the Connecticut State
9 University System;
10 (3) One appointed by the majority leader of the House of
11 Representatives, who is a member of the faculty at a nonprofit,
12 independent institution of higher education in the state;
13 (4) One appointed by the majority leader of the Senate, who is a
14 member of the faculty at The University of Connecticut;
15 (5) One appointed by the minority leader of the House of
16 Representatives, who is a member of the faculty at a nonprofit,

47 (c) All initial appointments to the commission pursuant to
48 subdivisions (1) to (11), inclusive, of subsection (b) of this section shall
49 be made not later than September 1, 2026, and shall terminate on June
50 30, 2028, and June 30, 2029, in accordance with the provisions of
51 subsection (f) of this section, regardless of when the initial appointment
52 was made. Any member of the commission may serve more than one
53 term.
54 (d) The vice presidents of research at The University of Connecticut
55 and Yale University shall serve as the cochairpersons of the commission.
56 Such cochairpersons shall schedule the first meeting of the commission,
57 which shall be held not later than September 1, 2026.
58 (e) The administrative staff of the Office of Higher Education shall
59 serve as administrative staff of the commission.
60 (f) Appointed members of the commission shall serve for two-year
61 terms which shall commence on the date of appointment, except as
62 provided in subsection (c) of this section, and members first appointed
63 shall have the following terms: (1) Members appointed by the Governor,
64 the majority leader of the House of Representatives, the minority leader
65 of the House of Representatives and the chairpersons of the joint
66 standing committee of the General Assembly having cognizance of
67 matters relating to higher education and employment advancement
68 shall initially serve a term of two years, and (2) members appointed by
69 the speaker of the House of Representatives, the president pro tempore
70 of the Senate, the majority leader of the Senate, the minority leader of
71 the Senate and the ranking members of the joint standing committee of
72 the General Assembly having cognizance of matters relating to higher
73 education and employment advancement shall initially serve a term of
74 three years. Members shall continue to serve until their successors are
75 appointed.
76 (g) Any vacancy shall be filled by the appointing authority. Any
77 vacancy occurring other than by expiration of term shall be filled for the
78 balance of the unexpired term.

79 (h) A majority of the commission shall constitute a quorum for the
80 transaction of any business.
81 (i) The members of the commission shall serve without
82 compensation, but shall, within the limits of available funds, be
83 reimbursed for expenses necessarily incurred in the performance of
84 their duties.
85 (j) The commission shall have the following powers and duties: (1)
86 Award research funding grants to research faculty at institutions of
87 higher education in the state, prioritizing grants to fund research
88 conducted on topics and subjects that have been reduced, restricted or
89 eliminated as a result of changes in the funding of grants from the
90 National Institutes of Health, National Science Foundation and other
91 federal agencies; (2) obtain from any executive department, board,
92 commission or other agency of the state such assistance and data as
93 necessary and available to carry out the purposes of this section; (3)
94 accept any gift, donation or bequest for the purpose of performing the
95 duties described in this section; (4) establish bylaws to govern its
96 procedures; and (5) perform such other acts as may be necessary and
97 appropriate to carry out the duties described in this section.
98 (k) The commission shall meet as often as deemed necessary by the
99 cochairpersons of the commission. Any appointed member who fails to
100 attend three consecutive meetings or who fails to attend fifty per cent of
101 all meetings held during any calendar year shall be deemed to have
102 resigned from the commission.
103 (l) Not later than January 1, 2028, and annually thereafter, the
104 commission shall submit a report, in accordance with the provisions of
105 section 11-4a of the general statutes, to the Governor, the Commissioner
106 of Higher Education and the joint standing committee of the General
107 Assembly having cognizance of matters relating to higher education
108 and employment advancement on the research funding grants awarded
109 by the commission during the prior fiscal year, which shall include, but
110 need not be limited to, (1) the number and amount of research funding
