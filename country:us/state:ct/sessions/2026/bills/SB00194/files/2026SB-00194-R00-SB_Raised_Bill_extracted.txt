General Assembly Raised Bill No. 194
February Session, 2026 LCO No. 1159
Referred to Committee on PUBLIC HEALTH
Introduced by:
(PH)
AN ACT CONCERNING CARDIAC SCREENING AND SUDDEN
CARDIAC ARREST PREVENTION FOR CERTAIN STUDENT
ATHLETES.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General
Assembly convened:
1 Section 1. Section 10-149f of the general statutes is repealed and the
2 following is substituted in lieu thereof (Effective July 1, 2026):
3 (a) For purposes of this section and section 10-149g, (1) "intramural
4 or interscholastic athletics" shall include any activity sponsored by a
5 school or local education agency, as defined in section 10-15f, or an
6 organization sanctioned by the local education agency that involves any
7 athletic contest, practice, scrimmage, competition, demonstration,
8 display or club activity, and (2) "high-intensity endurance or power
9 sport" means football, basketball, soccer, hockey, lacrosse and a middle-
10 distance or long-distance track event.
11 (b) For the school year commencing July 1, 2015, and each school year
12 thereafter, the State Board of Education, in consultation with (1) the
13 Commissioner of Public Health, (2) the governing authority for
14 intramural and interscholastic athletics, (3) an appropriate organization

28 cardiac arrest to return to intramural or interscholastic athletics. When
29 developing or approving such program, the State Board of Education
30 may utilize existing materials developed by organizations such as
31 Simon's Fund.
32 (c) (1) On or before July 1, 2015, the State Board of Education, in
33 consultation with the organizations described in subdivisions (1) to (4),
34 inclusive, of subsection (b) of this section, shall develop and approve an
35 informed consent form to distribute to the parents and legal guardians
36 of students involved in intramural or interscholastic athletics regarding
37 sudden cardiac arrest. Such informed consent form shall include, at a
38 minimum, (A) a summary of the sudden cardiac arrest awareness
39 education program described in subsection (b) of this section, and (B) a
40 summary of the applicable local or regional board of education's policies
41 regarding sudden cardiac arrests.
42 (2) For the school year commencing July 1, 2015, and each school year
43 thereafter, any person who holds or is issued a coaching permit by the
44 State Board of Education and is a coach of intramural or interscholastic
45 athletics shall, prior to commencing the coaching assignment for the
46 season of such school athletics, provide each participating student's
47 parent or legal guardian with a copy of the informed consent form
