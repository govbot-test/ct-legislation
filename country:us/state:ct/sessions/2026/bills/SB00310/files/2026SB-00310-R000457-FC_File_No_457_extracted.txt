AN ACT CONCERNING THE ESTABLISHMENT OF A PILOT
PROGRAM TO TRAIN CERTIFIED WASTEWATER TREATMENT
FACILITY OPERATORS.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General
Assembly convened:
1 Section 1. (Effective July 1, 2026) For the school year commencing July
2 1, 2026, the executive director of the Technical Education and Career
3 System shall establish a pilot program for the provision of one or more
4 courses to train students to become certified wastewater treatment
5 facility operators at technical education and career schools throughout
6 the state. Not later than January 1, 2027, the executive director shall
7 submit a report, in accordance with the provisions of section 11-4a of the
8 general statutes, to the joint standing committee of the General
9 Assembly having cognizance of matters relating to education. Such
10 report shall include the number of students who enrolled in such course
11 or courses, the timeframe in which such students may become certified
12 wastewater treatment facility operators after completion of such course
13 and recommendations for any funding or legislation to expand such
14 pilot program or to make such pilot program permanent.

Explanation
The bill results in a one-time cost of approximately $18,000 in FY 27
to the Connecticut Technical Education and Career System (CTECS).
The bill requires CTECS to conduct a pilot program to train students to
become certified wastewater treatment facility operators. The cost is
associated with additional staff hours worked and supplies as well as
materials needed to administer the program.
The Out Years
There is no fiscal impact in the out years, as the bill specifies that the
pilot program is for FY 27 only.
