AN ACT REDUCING TESTING BURDENS AND ENHANCING THE
MATH CURRICULUM.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General
Assembly convened:
1 Section 1. (NEW) (Effective July 1, 2026) (a) As used in this section,
2 "broad assessment" means an assessment that measures what students
3 should have learned during a semester or during a school year. "Broad
4 assessment" does not include the state-wide mastery examinations
5 under section 10-14n of the general statutes or any other student
6 assessments required by state or federal law.
7 (b) Not later than July 1, 2027, the Commissioner of Education shall
8 establish, within available appropriations, an incentive program for
9 school districts that (1) reduces or limits the amount of time students
10 spend on taking broad assessments during the school year, (2) integrates
11 state-provided interim and formative assessment tools into the local
12 curriculum in a manner that supports ongoing instructional
13 improvement, and (3) increases teacher competency in the formative
14 assessment process. The incentive program may include public

19 Sec. 2. (NEW) (Effective July 1, 2026) Not later than June 1, 2027, the
20 Department of Education shall develop and provide guidance to local

31 pursuant to the Elementary and Secondary Education Act of 1965, 20
32 USC 6301, et seq., as amended from time to time, and reauthorized by
33 the Every Student Succeeds Act, P.L. 114-95. Such request may include
34 a waiver from federal accountability requirements under said act that
35 allows the Department of Education to modify the state's high school
36 accountability model by reducing the weight assigned to the academic
37 achievement indicator and increasing the weight for other relevant
38 indicators, such as college and career-oriented measures.
39 Sec. 4. (Effective from passage) (a) Not later than January 1, 2027, the
40 Commissioner of Education shall redesign Connecticut's high school
41 mathematics pathways to prepare students for relevant postsecondary
42 careers. Such mathematics pathways shall include, but need not be
43 limited to, (1) science, technology, engineering and mathematics, (2)
44 data science and statistics, (3) quantitative reasoning, and (4) workforce
45 and applied mathematics. The commissioner may consult with relevant
