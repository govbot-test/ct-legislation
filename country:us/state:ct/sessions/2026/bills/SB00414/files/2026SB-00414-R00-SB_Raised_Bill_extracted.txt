AN ACT CONCERNING VEGETATION MANAGEMENT GUIDELINES
BY THE DEPARTMENT OF TRANSPORTATION.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General
Assembly convened:
1 Section 1. Section 13b-31h of the general statutes is repealed and the
2 following is substituted in lieu thereof (Effective October 1, 2026):
3 (a) The Department of Transportation shall develop, and thereafter
4 revise as necessary, guidelines governing tree and vegetation
5 management, removal and replacement along state highways for use by
6 its employees and contractors when undertaking maintenance and
7 construction projects. The goal of the guidelines shall be to ensure the
8 impacts of maintenance and construction projects on the environment,
9 landscape and noise pollution are balanced or outweighed by measures
10 taken to avoid and minimize the impacts.
11 (b) Such guidelines shall include, but need not be limited to,
12 provisions addressing (1) the safety of the traveling public; (2) general
13 roadside vegetation management activities performed by the
14 department, including, but not limited to, mowing, herbicide
15 application, grassing, replanting with native species whenever

17 beautification, enhancements and the effect on scenic roads designated

20 tree, brush or plant species' growth and impact, (B) storm water run-off,

51 Sec. 2. Section 63 of public act 24-151 is repealed and the following is
52 substituted in lieu thereof (Effective July 1, 2026):

This act shall take effect as follows and shall amend the following
sections:
Section 1 October 1, 2026 13b-31h
Sec. 2 July 1, 2026 PA 24-151, Sec. 63
Statement of Purpose:
To require the Commissioner of Transportation to consider the carbon
sequestration by trees and other vegetation along highways and
determine if revisions to the guidelines governing tree and vegetation
management by the Department of Transportation are necessary.
