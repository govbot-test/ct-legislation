Explanation
The bill prohibits striped bass fishing in the state between December
1 and March 31 annually and establishes graduated penalties for
violating any regulation regarding striped bass taking. This could result
in a minimal revenue gain to municipalities where the violations occur.
The bill imposes a fine of $150 for a first offense, $350 for a second
offense, and $500 for subsequent offenses. Fines for infractions must be
paid by mail to the municipality where the infraction occurred.
The Out Years
The annualized ongoing fiscal impact identified above would
continue into the future subject to the number of violations.

AN ACT CONCERNING THE REGULATION OF STRIPED BASS
FISHING IN THE STATE.
SUMMARY
This bill prohibits striped bass fishing (including catch and release)
in Connecticut between December 1 and March 31 annually. The bill
makes it an infraction to violate any regulation on striped bass taking
(killing, fishing, or other means of trapping) and imposes a fine of (1)
$150 for a first offense, (2) $350 for a second offense, and (3) $500 for
subsequent offenses. Under the bill, fines for this infraction must be paid
by mail to the municipality where the infraction occurred.
EFFECTIVE DATE: October 1, 2026, except the provision on the
infraction is effective upon passage.
