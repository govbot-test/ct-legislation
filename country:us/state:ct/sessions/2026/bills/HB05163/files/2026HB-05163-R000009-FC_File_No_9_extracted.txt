The Committee on Children reported through REP. PARIS of
the 145th Dist., Chairperson of the Committee on the part of the

AN ACT ESTABLISHING A TASK FORCE TO STUDY METHODS AND
PROGRAMS FOR IMPROVING AFFORDABILITY FOR CHILDREN,
FAMILIES AND YOUNG PROFESSIONALS.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General
Assembly convened:
1 Section 1. (Effective from passage) (a) There is established a task force
2 to (1) study methods and programs for improving the affordability of
3 the state for children, families and young professionals, including, but
4 not limited to, cash assistance programs, educational and employment
5 training programs and programs to assist families in paying for and
6 reducing costs associated with child care services, (2) analyze the
7 efficacy of such methods and programs and provide recommendations
8 for the implementation of such methods and programs on a state-wide
9 basis, (3) study systemic barriers that prevent improvements to
10 affordability, including, but not limited to, housing policies,
11 transportation access, health care access, child care access and racial
12 disparities, (4) study the ways in which inflation and regional cost
13 differences impact affordability across the state, (5) develop a method
14 for tracking improvements in income, home ownership, job retention

15 and other markers of economic mobility in areas of the state with low
16 affordability, (6) study the availability of educational, employment and
17 child care programs in areas of the state with low affordability and
18 analyze gaps in such availability to determine where and how to meet
19 the need for such programs, (7) identify any policies and programs,

45 (8) Not more than ten members jointly appointed by the
46 cochairpersons of the task force, each of whom shall (A) have expertise
47 in nonprofit administration, business administration, economics,
48 education, housing, child welfare, child care, labor, workforce
49 development or social services administration, or (B) be a representative

52 subdivision (7) or (8) of subsection (b) of this section may be a member

54 (d) All initial appointments to the task force shall be made not later
55 than thirty days after the effective date of this section. Any vacancy shall

59 appropriations, or the chairpersons' designees, shall serve as
60 cochairpersons of the task force. Such cochairpersons shall schedule
61 each meeting of the task force, the first of which shall be held not later
62 than thirty days after the effective date of this section.
63 (f) The administrative staff of the joint standing committee of the
64 General Assembly having cognizance of matters relating to children
65 shall serve as administrative staff of the task force.
66 (g) Not later than June 1, 2027, the cochairpersons shall submit a
67 preliminary report on the findings and recommendations of the task
68 force to the joint standing committees of the General Assembly having
69 cognizance of matters relating to children, human services and
70 appropriations, in accordance with the provisions of section 11-4a of the
71 general statutes.
72 (h) Not later than July 1, 2027, the cochairpersons shall hold a public
73 forum, at which the cochairpersons shall present the preliminary report
74 submitted pursuant to subsection (g) of this section and solicit public
75 comment.

OFA Fiscal Note
State Impact: None
Municipal Impact: None
Explanation
The bill establishes a task force to study the state's affordability for
children, families, and young professionals through the analysis of cash
assistance, childcare, and systemic barriers. There is no fiscal impact
because the participating agencies already possess the necessary
expertise to fulfill these requirements.

State Impact: None
