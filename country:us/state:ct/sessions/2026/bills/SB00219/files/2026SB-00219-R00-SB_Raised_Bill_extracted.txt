February Session, 2026 LCO No. 1568
Referred to Committee on BANKING
Introduced by:
(BA)
AN ACT CONCERNING CIVIL PENALTIES FOR CERTAIN
VIOLATIONS RELATING TO RENTAL SECURITY DEPOSITS.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General
Assembly convened:
1 Section 1. Subdivision (1) of subsection (j) of section 47a-21 of the 2026
2 supplement to the general statutes is repealed and the following is
3 substituted in lieu thereof (Effective October 1, 2026):
4 (j) (1) Except as provided in subdivision (2) of this subsection, the
5 commissioner may receive and investigate complaints regarding any
6 alleged violation of [subsections] subsection (b), (d), (h) or (i) of this
7 section. For the purposes of such investigation, any person who is or
8 was a landlord shall be subject to the provisions of section 36a-17. If the
9 commissioner determines that any landlord has violated any provision
10 of this section over which the commissioner has jurisdiction, the
11 commissioner may, in accordance with [section] sections 36a-50 and
12 36a-52, order such [person] landlord to pay a civil penalty not exceeding
13 one hundred thousand dollars per violation, cease and desist from such
14 practices and [to] comply with the provisions of this section.

Section 1 October 1, 2026 47a-21(j)(1)
Statement of Purpose:
To establish that if the Banking Commissioner determines that any
landlord has violated certain provisions concerning rental security
deposits, the commissioner may, in accordance with sections 36a-50 and
36a-52 of the general statutes, order such landlord to pay a civil penalty
not exceeding one hundred thousand dollars per violation, cease and
desist from such practices and comply with certain provisions
concerning rental security deposits.
