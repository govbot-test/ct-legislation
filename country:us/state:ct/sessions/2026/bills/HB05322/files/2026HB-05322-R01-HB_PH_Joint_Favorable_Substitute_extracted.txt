General Assembly Substitute Bill No. 5322
February Session, 2026
AN ACT CONCERNING A WORKING GROUP REGARDING
ENDOMETRIOSIS.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General
Assembly convened:
1 Section 1. (NEW) (Effective from passage) (a) There is established a
2 working group regarding endometriosis for the purpose of evaluating
3 and making recommendations regarding the diagnosis, treatment,
4 research, education and public awareness of endometriosis in the state.
5 The working group shall be within the Legislative Department. The
6 working group shall evaluate the following:
7 (1) The prevalence and impact of endometriosis on residents of the
8 state;
9 (2) Barriers to timely and accurate diagnosis of endometriosis;
10 (3) Access to evidence-based treatment for endometriosis, including,
11 but not limited to, medical, surgical and therapeutic interventions;
12 (4) Insurance coverage and reimbursement practices for the
13 treatment of endometriosis;
14 (5) The impact of endometriosis in the workplace, including, but not
15 limited to, leave, accommodations and employment protections;

16 (6) Gaps in public and provider education and training concerning
17 endometriosis; and
18 (7) Opportunities to improve endometriosis data collection, research
19 initiatives and patient outcomes.
20 (b) The working group shall consist of the following members, who
21 shall be appointed not later than thirty days after the effective date of
22 this section:
23 (1) Four appointed by the speaker of the House of Representatives,
24 one of whom shall be a member of the House or Representatives, one of
25 whom shall be a physician licensed pursuant to chapter 370 of the
26 general statutes with demonstrated experience in the diagnosis and
27 treatment of endometriosis, one of whom shall be a representative of a
28 federally qualified health center and one of whom shall be an individual
29 residing in the state who has been diagnosed with endometriosis;
30 (2) Four appointed by the president pro tempore of the Senate, one of
31 whom shall be a member of the Senate, one of whom shall be a physician
32 licensed pursuant to chapter 370 of the general statutes who is a member
33 of the American College of Obstetricians and Gynecologists, one of
34 whom shall be a researcher affiliated with an academic or research
35 institution in the state with expertise in endometriosis and one of whom
36 shall be a patient advocate with experience advocating on behalf of
37 individuals with endometriosis;
38 (3) Four appointed by the minority leader of the House of
39 Representatives, one of whom shall be a member of the House of
40 Representatives, one of whom shall be a pediatric or an adolescent
41 medicine physician licensed pursuant to chapter 370 of the general
42 statutes and currently practicing in the state, one of whom shall be an
43 individual in the state with expertise in racial and health equity or who
44 represents a community-based organization serving historically
45 underserved populations and one of whom shall be a representative of
46 an association of hospitals in the state or an administrator of a hospital
47 in the state;
