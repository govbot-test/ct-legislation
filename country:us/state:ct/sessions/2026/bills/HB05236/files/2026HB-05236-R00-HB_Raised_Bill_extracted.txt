AN ACT CONCERNING WORKPLACE HEALTH, SAFETY AND
VIOLENCE PREVENTION FOR PUBLIC TRANSIT WORKERS.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General
Assembly convened:
1 Section 1. (NEW) (Effective October 1, 2026) (a) (1) There is established
2 a Public Transit Workplace Health, Safety and Violence Prevention
3 Committee, which shall be in the Department of Transportation for
4 administrative purposes only. The committee shall consist of (A) two
5 public transit bus operators from each transit district formed under
6 chapter 103a of the general statutes appointed pursuant to subdivision
7 (2) of this subsection, and (B) two representatives of management from
8 each transit district appointed by the board of directors of such transit
9 district. The Commissioner of Transportation, or the commissioner's
10 designee, shall be an ex-officio, nonvoting member of the committee.
11 (2) If public transit bus operators employed by a transit district are
12 members of a collective bargaining unit, the employee organization
13 designated as the exclusive bargaining representative for such unit shall
14 appoint the two public transit bus operators to such committee. If public
15 transit bus operators are not members of a collective bargaining unit,

132 Sec. 3. (NEW) (Effective October 1, 2026) (a) As used in this section,
133 "security barrier" means a protective partition made of hard, durable
134 and bulletproof materials designed to shield a public transit bus
135 operator from physical assault or projectiles while maintaining visibility
136 and communication with passengers that (1) extends from the bus floor
137 to the bus ceiling, (2) is capable of fully enclosing the public transit bus
138 operator's workstation and fully preventing the unwanted entry of
139 persons, limbs, fluids and objects into such workstation, and (3) does
