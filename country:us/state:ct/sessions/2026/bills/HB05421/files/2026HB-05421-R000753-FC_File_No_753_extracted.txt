AN ACT ESTABLISHING AN ACCOUNT TO PROVIDE PATIENT LIFTS
TO CERTAIN HEALTH CARE OFFICES AND FACILITIES.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General
Assembly convened:
1 Section 1. (NEW) (Effective July 1, 2026) (a) There is established a
2 separate, nonlapsing account to be known as the "health care facility
3 patient lift account". The account shall contain (1) any amounts
4 appropriated or otherwise made available by the state for the purposes
5 of this section, (2) any moneys required by law to be deposited in the
6 account, and (3) gifts, grants, donations or bequests made for the
7 purposes of this section. Investment earnings credited to the assets of
8 the account shall become part of the assets of the account. The State
9 Treasurer shall administer the account.
10 (b) All moneys deposited in the account shall be used by the
11 Department of Public Health for the purposes of this section. The
12 Commissioner of Public Health may deduct and retain from the moneys
13 in the account an amount equal to the costs incurred by the department

15 not exceed two per cent of the balance of the account in any fiscal year.

State Impact: None
Municipal Impact: None
Explanation
The bill establishes a separate, nonlapsing grant account to support
health care facility purchases of patient lifts, resulting in no fiscal impact
to the state. The Department of Public Health has sufficient resources to
administer the account.
House "A" limits the grant account established by the bill to
purchases of patient lifts and expands eligible grantees to include
physician practices, radiological facilities, and imaging centers. These
changes have no fiscal impact.

OFA Bill Analysis
sHB 5421
AN ACT ESTABLISHING AN ACCOUNT TO PROVIDE DURABLE
MEDICAL EQUIPMENT TO HEALTH CARE FACILITIES.
SUMMARY:
The bill creates the health care facility patient lift account as a
separate, nonlapsing account administered by the State Treasurer.
The account must contain: (1) relevant appropriations or funding
otherwise made available by the state; (2) any money required by law to
be deposited in the account; and (3) relevant gifts, grants, donations or
bequests. Any investment earnings credited to the assets of the account
must also be included in the assets of the account.
Under the bill, the Department of Public Health (DPH) must use the
account’s funds to provide grants to licensed physician offices,
radiological facilities, imaging centers or health care facilities to
purchase patient lifts. These lifts are to be used to transfer elderly
persons, persons with disabilities or persons with injuries or chronic
health conditions from facility beds to wheelchairs or other equipment.
DPH may deduct and retain an amount equal to the costs incurred to
award such grants, up to two percent of the account's balance annually.
EFFECTIVE DATE: July 1, 2026
