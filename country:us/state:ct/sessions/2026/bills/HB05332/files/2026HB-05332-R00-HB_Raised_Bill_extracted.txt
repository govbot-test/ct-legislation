AN ACT CONCERNING CERTAIN BOATING REGULATIONS.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General
Assembly convened:
1 Section 1. (NEW) (Effective October 1, 2026) No person shall extend
2 any limb over the gunwale of a vessel while such vessel is in operation.
3 Any person who violates the provisions of this section shall be fined one
4 hundred dollars.
5 Sec. 2. Subsection (m) of section 15-140c of the general statutes is
6 repealed and the following is substituted in lieu thereof (Effective October
7 1, 2026):
8 (m) Any person who abandons a vessel shall have committed a
9 violation and shall be fined not less than [three hundred] seven hundred
10 fifty dollars nor more than [five hundred] one thousand dollars, except
11 if such abandonment occurred during a declared emergency and as a
12 result of the conditions that caused such emergency to be declared.
13 Notwithstanding the provisions of this section, the Commissioner of
14 Energy and Environmental Protection may authorize the recovery of
15 reasonable costs against the last owner of record of a vessel for the
16 recovery of an abandoned vessel and any associated environmental

Statement of Purpose:
To prohibit the act of extending one's limbs over the gunwale of a vessel
while it is in operation, increase the abandoned vessel fine and authorize
the recovery of costs associated with abandoned vessels for state and
local law enforcement agencies.
