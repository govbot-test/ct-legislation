AN ACT CONCERNING ASSORTED REVISIONS TO THE EDUCATION
STATUTES.
Be it enacted by the Senate and House of Representatives in General
Assembly convened:
1 Section 1. (NEW) (Effective July 1, 2026) (a) The Department of
2 Education may approve one or more supplemental literacy support
3 products that utilizes a machine-learning or other machine-based
4 system capable of (1) listening to students read aloud, (2) analyzing
5 student reading performance in real time, and (3) providing immediate,
6 individualized support or intervention in the form of tutoring and
7 instructional feedback.
8 (b) The department shall approve a product pursuant to this section
9 only if such product (1) aligns with the science of reading that is
10 supported by a substantial body of research or evidence demonstrating
11 such alignment, (2) is capable of aligning with and supporting literacy
12 curricula selected by a local or regional board of education or by the
13 Department of Education, (3) may be used by school districts as part of
14 state-wide literacy initiatives, including, but not limited to, the state's
15 high-dosage tutoring initiative and other evidence-based literacy

29 Sec. 3. (NEW) (Effective January 1, 2027) (a) The Department of
30 Education shall establish an American Sign Language education
31 working group which shall consist of (1) a representative from each of
32 the following: The Departments of Education and Aging and Disability
33 Services, the Labor Department, the Office of Higher Education, the
34 Connecticut Council of Language Teachers, the American School for the
35 Deaf and the Connecticut Association of the Deaf, and (2) at least one
36 American Sign Language instructor who teaches in a public school in
37 the state.
38 (b) The working group shall provide recommendations to the
39 Department of Education regarding the following:
40 (1) Curriculum guidance for American Sign Language instruction
41 that includes, but is not limited to, aligning such curriculum with
42 nationally recognized proficiency frameworks;
43 (2) Teacher certification standards based on standards established by
44 the American Sign Language Teachers Association and the American
45 Sign Language Proficiency Interview that includes, but is not limited to,
