Explanation
The bill results in a General Fund (GF) revenue loss to the
Department of Emergency Services and Public Protection (DESPP) in FY
27 through FY 29 by requiring the agency to temporarily waive the $75
criminal history records fee and related pre-registration fees for anyone
applying for a pardon. (Under current law DESPP may waive the $75
fee only for indigent individuals.) Revenue loss from the bill would
depend on the additional waivers issued and number of people
applying for a pardon.1
The Out Years
Beginning in FY 30, the GF revenue impact is reduced by reverting
eligibility for the criminal history fee waiver back to only those who are
indigent. Also beginning in FY 30, there is a potential cost to DESPP by
requiring the agency to reimburse independent contractors for the $30
service fee they would otherwise charge for those who are indigent.
1 As a point of reference, the Connecticut Board of Pardons and Paroles reviewed 2,032
applications for pardon consideration in FY 25. It is unknown how many of those
applicants received the current indigency fee waiver. The potential maximum revenue
loss associated with this number of pardon requests is $152,400 ($75 x 2,032).

OLR Bill Analysis
SB 343
AN ACT CONCERNING FEES CHARGED FOR CRIMINAL HISTORY
RECORDS CHECKS.
SUMMARY
This bill waives certain fingerprinting and criminal history record
search and pre-registration fees for individuals requesting them for their
pardon applications.
Current law allows the Department of Emergency Services and
Public Protection (DESPP) to waive the $75 fee for a criminal history
report for indigent individuals requesting it for their pardon
application. The bill instead requires DESPP, up until July 1, 2029, to
waive the fee for anyone applying for a pardon before reverting to
current law.
The bill also waives the following fees up until July 1, 2029, for
anyone applying for a pardon and on and after that date for indigent
individuals applying for a pardon:
1. the convenience fee for fingerprinting of up to $30 charged by
independent contractors operating under an agreement with
DESPP,
2. any processing or distribution fees charged for a criminal history
report, and
3. any fee for electronically pre-registering with the Connecticut
criminal history request system.
Under the bill, an individual seeking these fingerprinting or
background check services on or after July 1, 2029, for their pardon
application may apply for a fee waiver due to indigency on a DESPP-
